## БАЗА ЗНАНЬ (Part 4):

[Topic 31: Modal Verbs (Must vs Have to)]
- Vocab (Full): Obligation=Зобов'язання; Forbidden=Заборонено; Optional=Необов'язковий; Chores=Домашні обов'язки; Deadline=Дедлайн; To keep up with=Встигати за.
- Grammar: Must (Personal feelings). Have to (Rules/Facts). Mustn't (Forbidden). Don't have to (Not necessary). Past: Had to. Future: Will have to.
- Practice: She must finish her homework; You have to study more; He must wake up early; They don’t have to visit that exhibition; We had to call the doctor; had to submit my report yesterday; must wear a uniform at work; will have to leave early; will have to adapt to new technologies; mustn’t forget your promises; We have to be at the meeting at 9; To be honest, I believe I must drink two liters of water; I’m afraid I have to wake up at 6 AM; I remember that I had to tidy my room; To be fair, it wasn't my favorite task; Could you please remember that we have to submit the weekly reports?; You mustn't use it without booking; You don't have to bring anything; I must check my schedule first.

[Topic 32: Health Problems & Medical Advice]
- Vocab (Full): To feel sick=Почуватися хворим; To have a cold=Мати застуду; To have the flu=Хворіти на грип; To sneeze=Чхати; To cough=Кашляти; Headache=Головний біль; Stomachache=Біль у шлунку; Earache=Біль у вусі; Toothache=Зубний біль; Sore throat=Біль у горлі; Allergy=Алергія.
- Grammar: Body part + ache (headache). Sore + body part (sore throat). Sick (informal/US) vs Ill (formal/UK).
- Practice: feel sick, it’s best to rest; avoid getting the flu; allergy season, I often sneeze; syrup helped reduce his cough; hot tea can calm down a sore throat; I got a headache; Eating something unhealthy gave her a stomachache; He couldn’t hear because of an earache; She couldn’t concentrate because of a toothache; Actually, I try to stay active; I’m afraid I haven’t been very disciplined lately; I would suggest that getting a flu vaccine is essential; I'm afraid I've developed a terrible toothache; Could you tell me if the pain is constant?; Actually, it hurts more; To be honest, I’ve been sneezing constantly; I would recommend taking some medicine.

[Topic 33: Can, Could, Be able to]
- Vocab (Full): Ability=Здатність / вміння; To count=Рахувати; To drive=Водити; To explain=Пояснювати; To attend=Відвідувати; To pass a test=Скласти іспит; Instruction=Інструкція.
- Grammar: Can (Present). Could (Past). Be able to (All tenses, especially future: will be able to).
- Practice: I can swim every day; She can’t drive; Can you explain this for me, please?; When I was 6, I could run very fast; She could speak French when she was a child; He couldn’t pass the test; My grandmother could play the piano; I am able to speak English well; She was able to finish her homework yesterday; They will be able to come tomorrow; We will not be able to attend the meeting; Was she able to understand the instructions?; I can help you now; We will be able to meet tomorrow; She was able to find her bag; Actually, I can ride a bike quite well; To be honest, I’m afraid I can’t; math was one of my favorite subjects, so I was always quite good; To be fair, I found arithmetic a bit challenging; I’m confident I’ll be able to communicate fluently.

[Topic 34: At the Doctor's]
- Vocab (Full): To feel well=Почуватися добре; To feel sick / ill=Почуватися хворим; To feel tired=Почуватися втомленим; To feel nervous=Хвилюватися; To have a pain in...=Відчувати біль у...; Stomachache=Біль у животі; Allergy=Алергія; Treatment=Лікування.
- Grammar: To feel + Adjective. To have a pain in + Body part. Should for advice.
- Practice: I feel tired in the morning; I feel ill!; My little sister feels sick, she has a stomachache; I always feel nervous when I tell the doctor; What should I do?; Where does it hurt?; I have an allergy to flowers; You have a pain in your back; Actually, I feel quite energetic today; To be honest, I feel okay, but I might be a little bit tired; I'm afraid I don't feel very well; I believe it would be best to stay at home; you might want to consider making an appointment; I was wondering if it would be possible to see a doctor?; Actually, I have a persistent pain.

[Topic 35: Cooking Without Stress]
- Vocab (Full): Rice noodles=Рисова локшина; Chicken breast=Куряча грудка; Garlic=Часник; Broccoli=Броколі; Olive oil=Оливкова олія; Soy sauce=Соєвий соус; Salt & Pepper=Сіль та перець; Bowl=Миска; Pan=Сковорідка; Knife=Ніж; To pour=Наливати; To drain=Зливати воду; To slice=Нарізати скибочками; To mix=Змішувати; Delicious=Смачний; Tender=Ніжний.
- Grammar: Imperatives for instructions (Heat, Add, Pour). "Do not forget to...".
- Practice: drain the water from the noodles; Heat some olive oil; delicious and tender; add soy sauce and garlic; pour water into the pan; Use a knife to slice; Mix the ingredients well; To be honest, I absolutely love it; Actually, I don't mind it, but I prefer simple recipes; I’m afraid I’m not very good at it; homemade meals are much healthier; I would say my favourite is chicken stir-fry; To be fair, I enjoy both; I would prefer to eat out for special occasions; I definitely have a sweet tooth!; could you please pass me the knife?; would you mind draining the water?; Actually, it’s very simple.

[Topic 36: Mastering Too and Enough]
- Vocab (Full): Too=Занадто; Enough=Достатньо; Crowded=Переповнений; To react=Реагувати; To concentrate=Зосереджуватися; Yummy=Смачний; Rude=Грубий.
- Grammar: Too + Adjective. Adjective + Enough. Too many (countable). Too much (uncountable). Too few/little.
- Practice: The room is too cold for me; The book is too difficult; The noise is too loud to concentrate; The car is too expensive; there are too many people; He added too much pepper; We had too few chairs; I spent too little time preparing; She didn’t study enough; soup isn’t hot enough; It happened too quickly, so I didn't have time to react; I earn enough money; To be honest, it was too expensive for my budget; Actually, it wasn't practical enough; I’m afraid she isn’t tall enough; It seems she spent too little time preparing; I'm afraid this soup is too spicy; I think the water will be enough; I believe my skills are sufficient enough.

[Topic 37: Welcome to the restaurant!]
- Vocab (Full): A waiter / A waitress=Офіціант/ка; A reservation=Бронювання; To book a table=Забронювати столик; To be fully booked=Все зайнято; Bill / Check=Рахунок; Same for me=Мені теж саме; Service=Обслуговування; Order=Замовлення.
- Grammar: Ordering: "I will have...". Requests: "Can we get...?", "Can I have...?".
- Practice: Do you have a reservation?; I made a reservation for 7 PM; We are fully booked for tonight; I will have the chicken pasta; Same for me, please; I am sorry but my food is too cold; Excuse me, this is not what I ordered; Can we get the bill, please?; To be honest, I prefer eating out; Actually, I'd much rather cook at home; I'm quite fond of Italian restaurants; I’m afraid I’m a bit boring, but I actually love Japanese food; I'm afraid we haven't booked a table; Is there any chance you could fit us in?; I’m terribly sorry, but we are actually fully booked; I'm sorry to bother you, but I think there's been a slight mistake.

[Topic 39: Comparative & Superlative Adjectives]
- Vocab (Full): Expensive=Дорогий; Comfortable=Зручний; Interesting=Цікавий; Difficult / Hard=Важкий; Delicious / Tasty=Смачний; Exciting=Захоплюючий; Serious=Серйозний; Pretty=Гарний; Smart=Розумний; Fast=Швидкий.
