## 2. Grammar Essentials

На основі слайдів було виділено дві основні граматичні конструкції:

### A. Giving Advice with "Should"

Ми використовуємо **should** (порада) або **should not / shouldn't** (застереження), щоб допомогти комусь спланувати подорож.

* **Formula:** `Subject + should + verb (base form)`
* 
*Example:* "You **should book** a trip and decide where to stay".


* *Polite Note:* Щоб порада звучала менш нав'язливо, додавайте фрази-пом'якшувачі: *"I think you should..."* або *"Maybe you could..."*

### B. Present Perfect for Experiences

Для опису того, що людина вже зробила або ще не встигла зробити цього місяця/у житті.

* **Formula:** `Subject + have/has + Past Participle (V3)`
* 
*Positive:* "Mary **has packed** a suitcase".


* 
*Negative:* "Tom **hasn't gone** sightseeing".

Exercise 2: Multiple Choice 

1. Before we leave for our trip, we need to **book** a hotel for the weekend.
2. When I go to the beach, I love to **lie on a beach** and relax under the sun.
3. Make sure you have your **passport** with you when you travel to another country!
4. We decided to **travel** by train because it’s more comfortable than flying.
5. One of the best parts of traveling is **trying local food** from different cultures.
6. I always **pack** my suitcase the night before I leave for a trip.
7. Last summer, we went on a trip to Italy and **went sightseeing** at famous places like the Colosseum.
8. Don’t forget to buy a **ticket** before getting on the train.
9. My favorite way to relax during vacation is to **stay at a hotel** at a cozy resort.
10. Next month, I plan to **go on a trip** to the mountains with my family.

Exercise 3: Translation (Адаптовані завдання на переклад) 

1. *Ваш паспорт дійсний?* — **Is your passport valid?**

## 4. Speaking & Discussion Guide

*Питання зі слайдів та варіанти ввічливих розгорнутих відповідей.*

Q1: What two important things do you take on a trip? 

* **Positive/Neutral:** "To be honest, I believe a passport and a credit card are the most essential items. I might forget my sunglasses, but I definitely can't travel without my documents."
* **Detailed:** "Actually, it depends on the destination, but I’d say a smartphone for maps and a valid ticket are my top priorities."

Q2: When was the last time you went on a trip? Where did you go? 

* **Positive:** "I’m happy to say that I went to Italy last summer. I spent a wonderful week in Rome and went sightseeing almost every day."
* **Negative/Neutral:** "To be honest, I haven't been on a trip for a while because I've been quite busy with work, but I'm planning one for next year."

Q3: Do you usually pack your suitcase early, or do you leave it until the last minute? 

* **Early:** "I’m afraid I’m a bit of a perfectionist, so I usually start packing a few days in advance to make sure I don't forget anything."
* **Last minute:** "I must admit that I often leave it until the last minute. It's a bit stressful, but I somehow manage to get everything ready in time."

Q4: Do you prefer to travel by plane, train, or car? Why? 

* **Train:** "Personally, I’d much rather travel by train. It’s quite comfortable, and you get to enjoy the beautiful scenery through the window."
* **Plane:** "Actually, I prefer flying because it's much faster, although I'm aware it can be a bit tiring sometimes."

## 5. Scenario: Professional Travel Advice


**Situation: Planning a first-time trip.**

* **Client:** "I'm planning a trip to another country, but I'm a bit overwhelmed. What should I prepare?"
* **Assistant:** "I completely understand. **To be honest**, the first thing you should do is check if your passport is valid. Then, you **could** start by booking your tickets and a hotel. **I'd also suggest** packing your suitcase at least a day before the flight so you don't feel rushed."

## 6. Polite English Cheat Sheet


| Phrase | Usage |
| --- | --- |
| **"Actually..."** | Використовуйте, коли хочете делікатно виправити когось або висловити іншу думку. |
| **"To be honest..."** | Допомагає висловити свою чесну думку, навіть якщо вона не зовсім позитивна. |
| **"I'm afraid..."** | Ввічливий спосіб повідомити про проблему або розчарування (наприклад, "I'm afraid I forgot my ticket"). |
| **"Could / Might"** | Модальні дієслова, що роблять пропозиції менш директивними ("You might want to try local food"). |
| **"I'd suggest..."** | Більш професійна та м'яка форма замість простого "You should". |
