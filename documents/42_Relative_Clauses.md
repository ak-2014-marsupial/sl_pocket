## 3. Grammar Essentials

Relative Clauses are used to give more information about a person, thing, place, or time without starting a new sentence.

**Formula:** `[Noun] + [Relative Pronoun] + [Rest of the clause]`

* **Who** — used for people.
    *Example: The man **who** works at the theater is very kind.*
* **Which** — used for animals and objects.
    *Example: Tina has a bunch of flowers **which** her fan bought.*
* **That** — used for people, animals, and things (more informal).
    *Example: The movie **that** we watched last night was amazing.*
* **Whose** — used to show possession.
    *Example: Have you seen the girl **whose** favorite singer died last week?*
* **Where** — used for places.
    *Example: The arena **where** they performed was excellent.*
* **When** — used for time.
    *Example: That was the day **when** Ed Sheeran started the tour.*

**Types of Clauses:**
1.  **Defining:** Gives essential information. Without it, the sentence doesn't make sense.
    *Example: Here are the students **who** still need to finish their test.*
2.  **Non-Defining:** Gives extra information. We use commas to separate it.
    *Example: My brother, **who** lives in China, is visiting for Christmas.*

## 4. Written Practice

### Exercise 1: Fill in the blanks
1. The movie **that/which** we watched last night was amazing.
2. She is the doctor **who** saved my life.
3. I know a place **where** we can get great coffee.
4. The man **whose** car was stolen is very upset.
5. This is the year **when** we finally achieved our goal.

### Exercise 2: Combine the sentences
1. I have a friend. She speaks five languages. -> I have a friend **who** speaks five languages.
2. This is the hotel. We stayed here last summer. -> This is the hotel **where** we stayed last summer.
3. I bought a new phone. It has a great camera. -> I bought a new phone **which** has a great camera.
4. That’s the boy. His father is a famous actor. -> That’s the boy **whose** father is a famous actor.
5. We visited a museum. It has ancient artifacts. -> We visited a museum **which** has ancient artifacts.

### Translation Block
1. Це ресторан, де готують найкращу піцу в місті.
**This is the restaurant where they serve the best pizza in town.**
2. Я пам'ятаю день, коли ми вперше зустрілися.
**I remember the day when we first met.**
3. Вона вчителька, чиї уроки всі обожнюють.
**She’s the teacher whose classes everyone loves.**

## 5. Speaking & Discussion Guide

**Q: Who is a person you admire?**
* **Positive:** To be honest, I truly admire my former mentor **who** always encouraged me to push my boundaries. He is a person **whose** professional advice I still value today.
* **Neutral:** Actually, there isn't one specific person, but I generally respect people **who** manage to stay calm under pressure.
* **Negative:** I’m afraid I don’t have a specific role model right now, as it’s hard to find someone **whose** actions consistently match their words.

**Q: What is a hobby you enjoy?**
* **Positive:** I’m quite fond of photography, **which** allows me to capture beautiful moments. It’s a hobby **that** helps me relax after work.
* **Neutral:** I occasionally go swimming at the local gym **where** my friends also train. It's just a way to stay active.
* **Negative:** To be honest, I haven't found a hobby **that** I'm truly passionate about yet, though I’ve tried a few things.

**Q: What is a place you like to visit?**
* **Positive:** I love visiting the small mountain village **where** my grandparents live. It's a place **that** feels very peaceful.
* **Negative:** I’m afraid I don't enjoy crowded tourist spots **which** are usually too noisy for me. I prefer quiet locations.

## 6. Professional Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Describing a Colleague**
*Context: You are introducing a new team member to a client.*
* **Role A:** I’d like you to meet Sarah, **who** is our lead developer. She is the person **whose** team designed the new app interface.
* **Role B:** It’s a pleasure to meet you, Sarah. Could you tell me more about the features **that** you’ve implemented?

**Scenario 2: Explaining a Process**
*Context: Explaining how to use a new office tool.*
* **Role A:** This is the software **which** we use to track our project deadlines. 
* **Role B:** I see. Is this the place **where** I should upload my weekly reports?
* **Role A:** Actually, yes. It's a system **that** simplifies our communication significantly.

## 7. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | When to use it (Context) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Actually...** | To politely correct someone or add a specific detail. |
| **To be honest...** | To share a personal opinion or a slightly negative thought gently. |
| **I’m afraid...** | To deliver disappointing news or disagree politely. |
| **Could you... / Would you...** | To make requests sound more professional and less demanding. |
| **I was wondering if...** | To ask a question or make a request very indirectly. |
