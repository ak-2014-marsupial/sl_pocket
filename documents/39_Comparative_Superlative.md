### **2. Grammar Essentials**

#### **A. Comparative Form (Порівняльна форма)**

Використовується для порівняння двох речей. Після неї зазвичай іде сполучник **than** (ніж).

**One syllable (Один склад):** Adjective + **-er** (e.g., *Small → Smaller*).

**Two or more syllables (Два і більше складів):** **More** + Adjective (e.g., *Beautiful → More beautiful*).


#### **B. Superlative Form (Найвищий ступінь)**

Використовується для виділення одного об'єкта серед групи.

**One syllable:** **The** + Adjective + **-est** (e.g., *Tall → The tallest*).
 
**Two or more syllables:** **The most** + Adjective (e.g., *Interesting → The most interesting*).



#### **C. Spelling Rules** 

1. **Doubling Rule:** Якщо прикметник закінчується на "приголосний-голосний-приголосний", остання літера подвоюється (*Big → Bigger → The biggest*).
2. **-y Rule:** Якщо слово закінчується на **-y**, вона змінюється на **-i** (*Happy → Happier → The happiest*).

#### **D. Exceptions (Винятки)** 

* **Good** → Better → The best
* **Bad** → Worse → The worst
* **Far** → Farther/Further → The farthest/furthest
* **Little** → Less → The least
* **Many/Much** → More → The most

### **3. Written Practice**

**Exercise 1: Comparative Forms**

1. My house is **nicer** than yours.
2. She is **more beautiful** than her sister.
3. This chair is **more comfortable** than the one in the other room.
4. This task is **harder** than the previous one.
5. That movie was **more interesting** than the book.
6. Her new dress is **more expensive** than her old one.
7. His car is **faster** than mine.
8. This test is **more difficult** than the last one.

**Exercise 2: Superlative Forms**

1. He is **the tallest** student in the class.
2. This is **the most beautiful** place I have ever seen.
3. This is **the most expensive** restaurant in town.
4. This park is **the cleanest** in the city.
5. She is **the most active** when she is with her friends.
6. That was **the most stupid** joke I have ever heard.

### **4. Speaking & Discussion Guide**

**Q1: Who is taller, you or your best friend?**

* *Positive:* To be honest, my best friend is slightly taller than I am, although we are almost the same height.
* *Neutral:* Actually, I believe we are quite similar in height, though he might be just an inch taller.
* *Negative:* Unfortunately, I’m afraid I am significantly shorter than my best friend.

**Q2: What is the most beautiful place you have visited?**

* *Enthusiastic:* I’d have to say that Switzerland is the most beautiful place I’ve ever seen; the mountains are truly breathtaking.
* *Neutral:* That’s a difficult question, but perhaps the coast of Italy was the most picturesque location for me.

**Q3: What is the hardest subject you have studied in school?**

* *Polite/Direct:* To be perfectly honest, I found Advanced Mathematics to be the most challenging subject due to its complexity.
* *Softeners:* I’m afraid I wasn't very good at Physics; it was definitely the hardest subject for me to grasp.

### **5. Professional Practice Scenarios**

**Scenario 1: Tech Comparison (Laptop vs. Computer)**

* **A:** I’m thinking about buying this modern laptop. Would you say it’s better than my old desktop?
* **B:** Actually, it might be much faster and more convenient for travel, although your desktop could be more powerful for heavy tasks.

**Scenario 2: Real Estate Discussion (House A vs. House B)**

* **A:** Which house do you prefer?
* **B:** To be honest, House B is much more spacious, but House A is located in a significantly quieter neighborhood. I’m afraid House B might be too expensive for our current budget.

### **6. Polite English Cheat Sheet**

* **"Actually..." / "To be honest..."**: Використовуються, щоб м'яко висловити думку, яка може розходитися з очікуваннями співрозмовника.
* **"I’m afraid..."**: Використовується для ввічливого повідомлення поганих новин або висловлення незгоди (наприклад, "I'm afraid it's too expensive").
* **"Could you..." / "Would you say..."**: Модальні дієслова для пом'якшення запитань та прохань.
* **"I’d have to say..."**: Робить ваше твердження менш категоричним та більш розсудливим.
* **"Slightly" / "Significantly"**: Прислівники, що допомагають точніше та ввічливіше порівнювати речі, уникаючи різких оцінок.
