# The Language of Emotions and Feelings

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

*Нижче наведено список ключових слів з лекції з дефініціями англійською мовою та перекладом.*

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Excited** | Feeling very happy and enthusiastic about something. | В захваті / Радісно збуджений |
| **Nervous** | Feeling worried or anxious about something that is going to happen. | Знервований |
| **Upset** | Feeling unhappy, disappointed, or worried. | Засмучений |
| **Afraid** | Feeling fear or being frightened of something. | Наляканий |
| **Angry** | Having a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility. | Злий |
| **Tired** | Feeling that you need to rest or sleep. | Втомлений |
| **Awesome** | Extremely impressive or very good. | Чудовий / Класний |
| **Amazing** | Causing great surprise or wonder; excellent. | Дивовижний |
| **Awful** | Very bad or unpleasant. | Жахливий |

## 2. Grammar Essentials: Adjectives + Prepositions

*Важливо використовувати правильні прийменники після прикметників, що описують емоції.*

* **Happy / Excited / Nervous / Upset + ABOUT**

*Example:* I was so **happy about** winning the contest.
 
*Example:* He was **nervous about** his job interview.

* **Angry + WITH (someone) / ABOUT (something)**
 
*Example:* She was **angry with** her friend.

*Example:* She was **angry about** the unfair situation.

* **Afraid / Tired + OF**
 
*Example:* John is **afraid of** heights.
 
*Example:* I’m **tired of** doing the same tasks every day.

## 3. Written Practice (Completed Exercises)

*Всі вправи виконано повністю згідно з матеріалами слайдів.*

1. **Sentence Completion:**
* I was so **happy about** winning the contest that I couldn’t stop smiling all day.


* She was **upset about** losing her favorite book, so she spent hours looking for it.


* The view from the top of the mountain was **amazing**.


* The weather was **awful** during our trip, with heavy rain and strong winds all day.

* She was **nervous about** the test because she didn’t study enough.

2. **Dialogue Completion:**
* *Context: Exam stress*
**A:** "Hey, Tom! You don’t look so good. What’s wrong?"

**B:** "I’m really **nervous about** the exam tomorrow. I don’t think I studied enough.".

* *Context: Party feedback*
**A:** "Did you hear about the party last night? It was **awesome**!"

**B:** "Yes, I was **upset** that I couldn’t go. I had too much work to finish.".

## 4. Speaking & Discussion Guide

*Варіанти розгорнутих відповідей на дискусійні питання з використанням ввічливих конструкцій.*

### Q1: You hear good news about something important. 

How do you feel? 

* **Positive:** "Actually, I’d be absolutely **thrilled**. It would make me feel like all my hard work finally paid off."
* **Neutral:** "To be honest, I might feel a bit **relieved** at first, though I'd probably need a moment to let the news sink in."

### Q2: You’re waiting in a long line and it’s taking forever. 

How do you feel? 

* **Negative:** "I’m afraid I would get quite **frustrated**. I find it rather difficult to stay patient when I have a busy schedule."
* **Polite/Patient:** "I might feel a little **tired**, but I would probably try to distract myself with a podcast or some music."

Q3: Describe a situation where you felt nervous before an important event. 

* **Scenario:** "I remember feeling incredibly **nervous about** a presentation I had to give. To be honest, my hands were shaking. However, I managed to calm down by taking deep breaths, and it actually turned out much better than I had expected."

## 5. Professional Dialogue Scenarios

Scenario: A New Job Opportunity 

* **Manager:** "I heard you’re starting a new role next week. How are you feeling about the change?"
* **Employee:** "To be honest, I’m quite **excited about** it! It’s a wonderful opportunity. However, I must admit I’m also a little **nervous** since it’s a significant shift from my current responsibilities."
* **Manager:** "That’s perfectly natural. I’m sure you’ll do a great job."

## 6. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Softener / Construction | When to use it |
| --- | --- |
| **"To be honest..."** | Використовуйте, щоб пом'якшити пряму або потенційно негативну відповідь. |
| **"Actually..."** | Допомагає ввічливо уточнити інформацію або висловити свою думку. |
| **"I’m afraid..."** | Вживається для того, щоб ввічливо повідомити про щось неприємне (наприклад, про роздратування). |
| **"I might feel..."** | Використання модального дієслова *might* робить твердження менш категоричним. |
| **"A little / A bit..."** | Мінімізує негативне забарвлення прикметників (наприклад, *a little nervous*). |
