# Present Perfect (Ever, Never, Just, Already, Yet)

---

### 1. Vocabulary & Definitions


| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад українською |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Ever** | At any time in the past (used in questions). | Колись |
| **Never** | Not at any time in the past. | Ніколи |
| **Just** | A very short time ago. | Щойно |
| **Already** | Before now, or sooner than expected. | Вже |
| **Yet** | Up to the present time (used in negatives/questions). | Ще (не), вже |
| **To fail an exam** | To be unsuccessful in a test. | Провалити іспит |
| **To get a promotion** | To be raised to a higher rank or position at work. | Отримати підвищення |
| **Unusual food** | Something to eat that is not common or ordinary. | Незвичайна їжа |
| **To fall asleep** | To start sleeping, especially unintentionally. | Заснути |

### 2. Grammar Essentials: Present Perfect vs. Past Simple

Основна увага приділяється використанню часу для опису досвіду та результату, а не конкретного часу в минулому.

**The Formula:**

 
**Positive (+):** Subject + **have / has** + **Verb-ed / V3** 


 
**Negative (-):** Subject + **haven't / hasn't** + **Verb-ed / V3** 

 
**Question (?):** **Have / Has** + Subject + **Verb-ed / V3**? 



Key Differences :

* **Present Perfect:** Використовується для неозначеного часу, досвіду або коли важливий результат зараз (напр., *I have seen the Eiffel Tower*).
* **Past Simple:** Використовується для конкретного часу в минулому (напр., *I saw the Eiffel Tower in 2007*).

### 3. Written Practice (Completed Exercises)


**A. Open the brackets (Present Perfect):**

1. I **haven't eaten** sushi before, so I'm excited to try it tonight. 


2. **Have you met** a famous person? 


3. She **has finished** her homework, so now she can watch TV. 


4. They **haven't visited** Paris. 


5. We **have seen** that movie twice. 


6. He **has called**, so you can talk to him now. 


7. I **have decided** what to do. 


8. **Have you tried** the new restaurant in town? 



**B. Past Simple vs. Present Perfect:**

1. I **saw** that movie last week. 


2. She **hasn’t finished** her homework yet. 


3. We **met** in 2010 at a conference. 


4. They **have bought** a new car. 


5. I **lived** in London last summer. 


6. **Have you ever tried** sushi? 


7. He **didn’t call** me yesterday. 



**C. Translation Block (Correct the Mistakes):**

* *Incorrect:* I have saw this movie yesterday. → **Correct:** I saw this movie yesterday. 


* *Incorrect:* She have never been to Paris. → **Correct:** She has never been to Paris. 


* *Incorrect:* We didn’t have eaten lunch yet. → **Correct:** We haven’t eaten lunch yet.

### 4. Speaking & Discussion Guide

Нижче наведено відповіді на дискусійні питання зі слайдів у ввічливому стилі .

#### **Topic: Life Experiences (Have you ever...?)**

**1. Have you ever traveled to another country?**

* **Positive:** "Actually, I have been abroad several times. I find that exploring new cultures is quite rewarding, to be honest."
* **Negative:** "I’m afraid I haven’t had the chance to travel outside the country yet, though I would certainly love to in the future."
* **Neutral:** "I have visited a few neighboring countries, but I haven't traveled as much as I might have liked."

**2. Have you ever eaten a strange or unusual food?**

* **Positive:** "To be honest, I have tried some rather unusual street food while traveling. It was actually a very interesting experience."
* **Negative:** "I haven't really tried anything too exotic. I tend to stick to familiar dishes, if I'm being perfectly honest."

**3. Have you ever found money on the street?**

* **Neutral:** "I might have found a small coin once or twice, but nothing significant. I suppose I’m not particularly lucky in that regard!"

### 5. Professional Dialogue Scenarios

На основі вправ "Why is he/she...?" .

**Scenario 1: The Promotion (Professional Celebration)**

**Person A:** "You look absolutely thrilled! Could I ask what has happened?"

**Person B:** "To be honest, I’m quite excited because **I have just got a promotion**. I've worked quite hard for it, actually." 


**Person A:** "That is wonderful news! You certainly deserve it."

**Scenario 2: The Missed Meeting (Apology/Explanation)**

**Person A:** "I noticed you were a bit late for the briefing. Is everything alright?"
 
**Person B:** "I’m afraid **I have just missed the bus**. I would have been here on time otherwise. My apologies."

### 6. Polite English Cheat Sheet

Використовуйте ці фрази, щоб зробити свою англійську більш професійною та "м'якою".

| Softener / Construction | When to use | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Actually** | Для коректного уточнення або введення нової інформації. | "Actually, I have already finished the report." |
| **I’m afraid...** | Для ввічливого повідомлення поганих новин або відмови. | "I’m afraid I haven’t seen that email yet." |
| **To be honest / Honestly** | Для вираження особистої думки або зізнання. | "To be honest, I have never tried sushi." |
| **Could / Would / Might** | Модальні дієслова для пом’якшення прохань або припущень. | "Could you tell me if you have ever been there?" |
| **I suppose...** | Для висловлення невпевненого припущення. | "I suppose I might have forgotten the date." |
