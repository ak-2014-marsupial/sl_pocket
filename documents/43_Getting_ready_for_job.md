#  Getting ready for a job interview

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Apply for a job** |  To ask for a job, usually by sending your information to the company. |  Подати заявку на роботу  |
| **Job interview** |  A meeting where a person asks you questions about your skills, experience, and personality. |  Співбесіда на роботу  |
| **Job position** |  A specific role or job someone has at work, showing their tasks and responsibilities. |  Посада  |
| **Resume / CV** |  A document that lists your work experience, education, skills, and other information. |  Резюме  |
| **Offer a job** |  To give someone the chance to work for you by asking them to join your company. |  Запропонувати роботу  |
| **Salary** |  The money that a person is paid regularly for doing their job. |  Заробітна плата  |
| **Soft skills** |  Personal attributes like communication and teamwork that enable someone to interact effectively. | Гнучкі навички (особистісні якості) |
| **Hard skills** |  Specific, teachable abilities or skill sets that can be measured, such as programming. | Професійні (тверді) навички |
| **Strengths** |  Particular skills or beneficial qualities a person possesses. | Сильні сторони |
| **Weaknesses** |  Areas where a person may need improvement or lacks proficiency. | Слабкі сторони |
| **Degree** |  An academic rank conferred by a college or university after examination or completion of a course. | Диплом / Науковий ступінь |

## 2. Vocabulary Hooks
> "I decided to **apply** for the job to **supply** my skills to the team."
> "During the **interview**, I gave a clear **view** of my professional background."
> "A **salary** is the **glory** of a hard month's work."

## 3. Grammar Essentials
**Formula: Modal Verbs for Possibility and Ability (Can/Could/Might)**
 В англійській мові модальні дієслова використовуються для вираження вміння, можливості або ввічливого прохання під час співбесіди.
 *Example: **Could** you please tell me about your education?* 
 *Example: I **can** start next Monday.* 
*Example: My skills **might** be a good fit for this role.*

**Formula: Present Perfect for Experience**
 Використовується для опису досвіду, який ви набули до цього моменту, без зазначення конкретного часу.
 *Example: Have you **applied** for any jobs recently?* 
 *Example: I **have worked** at Star Hotel for five years.*

## 4. Written Practice

 I decided to **apply** for a new job at the tech company.
 She has a job **interview** scheduled for tomorrow afternoon.
 Make sure your **resume/CV** highlights your key achievements.
 The company decided to **offer** him the job after the interview.
 We discussed the **salary** during the final interview.
 She is known to be very **hard-working** and dedicated to her work.
 The team is looking for someone who is a **good** team player.
 Effective **time** management is important for meeting deadlines.
 She has a Bachelor’s **degree** in marketing.
 Programming is one of the essential **hard** skills for this job.
 Leadership and teamwork are important **soft** skills.

### Translation Block
1. Я хотів би подати заявку на цю посаду. — *I would like to **apply for this position**.*
2. Які ваші очікування щодо заробітної плати?  — *What are your **salary expectations**?* 
3. У мене є ступінь бакалавра з маркетингу.  — *I have a **Bachelor's degree** in marketing.* 

## 5. Speaking & Discussion Guide

 **Q: Tell me about yourself.** 
* **Positive:** To be honest, I’d be happy to. My name is Sarah, and I have a degree in marketing.  I consider myself a very hard-working person who enjoys working in a team.
* **Neutral:** Actually, I am from Toronto and have been working as a marketing assistant for two years.  I have strong communication skills.
* **Negative:** I’m afraid I don’t have much experience yet, but I am very eager to learn and improve my professional skills.

 **Q: What are your strengths and weaknesses?** 
* **Positive:** Well, I believe my main strengths are problem-solving and time management.  As for weaknesses, I sometimes take on too much work because I want to help others, but I am learning to prioritize better.
* **Neutral:** To be fair, I am a good team player and very dedicated.  However, I might need to improve my computer skills to be more efficient.
* **Negative:** It’s a bit difficult to say, but I sometimes struggle with public speaking, although I am actively working on it.

 **Q: Why do you want to work at this company?** 
* **Positive:** I admire your company’s attitude to customers and believe my experience would make me a good fit.  It is well-known for innovation, and I would love to grow here
*  **Neutral:** Actually, I heard about this position through a friend, and the team environment seems very professional and supportive.
* **Negative:** To be honest, I am looking for a new challenge where I can apply my hard skills in a more dynamic environment.

## 6. Professional Scenarios

 **Context:** Discussing professional experience and fit for a role .
**Scenario:**
* **Role A (Interviewer):** Good afternoon. Thank you for coming in for the **interview** today.
* **Role B (Candidate):** Good afternoon. Thank you for having me. I see you've looked at my **CV**.
* **Role A (Interviewer):** Yes, I see you have five years of experience. Why did you decide to **apply** for this **position**?
* **Role B (Candidate):** I admire your company’s innovation and believe my **skills** make me a good fit.

 **Context:** Finalizing terms and availability .
**Scenario:**
* **Role A (Interviewer):** Everything looks great. When **can you start**?
* **Role B (Candidate):** I can start in two weeks. And what are your **salary** expectations?
* **Role A (Interviewer):** We offer a competitive **salary** based on your skills. We will be in touch soon.

## 7. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | When to use it (Context) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Actually / To be honest** |  Use when you want to provide more detail or correct a point gently. |
| **I’m afraid / To be fair** |  Use to introduce a weakness or a limitation in a polite way. |
| **Could you please...?** |  A polite way to ask the interviewer for more information or clarification. |
| **I would be happy to...** | Use to show enthusiasm and willingness to provide information. |
| **I might need to...** |  Use when discussing areas for improvement without sounding too negative. |
