# 15. At the Store

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Shop assistant** | A person who helps customers in a store. | Продавець в магазині |
| **Customer** | A person who buys something in a store. | Покупець |
| **Fitting room** | A small room where you can try on clothes. | Примірочна |
| **Fit** | To be the right shape or size for someone. | Підходити по розміру |
| **Suit** | To look good on someone or match their style. | Пасувати по стилю / личити |
| **Receipt** | A piece of paper that shows what you bought and how much you paid. | Чек |
| **Bill** | A paper or request to pay for something (usually in a restaurant). | Рахунок |
| **Look for** | To try to find something or someone. | Шукати |
| **Try on** | To put on a piece of clothing to see if it fits. | Приміряти |


## 3. Grammar Essentials

**Formula: Present Continuous for Current Search**
`Subject + am/is/are + looking for + object`
Опис: Використовується для опису того, що ви шукаєте в магазині саме в цей момент.
*Example: Actually, I **am looking for** a red T-shirt.*

**Formula: Sentence Building (Fit vs Suit)**
`Subject + fit/suit + object`
Опис: Використовуємо **fit**, коли говоримо про розмір, та **suit**, коли говоримо про стиль чи колір.
*Example: This suit **fits** you (підходить за розміром).*
*Example: This sweater **suits** you (підходить до стилю/личить).*

**Formula: Polite Requests with Modals**
`Can / Could / May + I + verb`
Опис: Використовується для ввічливого прохання дозволу щось зробити.
*Example: **Could I** try this jacket on, please?*

## 5. Speaking & Discussion Guide

**Q: Do you prefer shopping online or in physical stores?**

* **Positive:** To be honest, I really enjoy physical stores because I can try things on in the **fitting room** to make sure they **fit** me perfectly.
* **Neutral:** Actually, it depends on what I am **looking for**. I buy electronics online, but I prefer going to the store for clothes.
* **Negative:** I’m afraid I find physical stores too crowded. I’d rather shop online where I can easily check if they have items in a **different color or size**.

**Q: What do you do if you buy something and then realize it doesn't suit you?**

* **Positive:** Actually, I usually take it back to the store. As long as I have the **receipt**, it’s quite easy to get a refund.
* **Negative:** To be honest, I often just keep it or give it to a friend, because I find returning items a bit stressful.
* **Neutral:** Perhaps I would try to exchange it for something else that **suits** my style better next time.

## 6. Professional Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Finding a Specific Item**
*Context: A customer is looking for professional attire.*

* **Role A (Shop Assistant):** Hello! How could I help you today?
* **Role B (Customer):** Excuse me, actually I’m looking for some black pants for a meeting.
* **Role A:** Certainly, they are right over there.

**Scenario 2: Checking Sizes**
*Context: A customer needs a different size.*

* **Role A (Customer):** Excuse me, would you happen to have this in a bigger size? This one doesn't **fit** me well.
* **Role B (Shop Assistant):** Let me check that for you... Yes, we have it in a Large.
* **Role A:** Thank you, I'll **try it on**.

**Scenario 3: Finalizing a Purchase**
*Context: At the checkout counter.*

* **Role A (Customer):** I think I'll **take** this shirt, it really **suits** me. How much is it?
* **Role B (Shop Assistant):** It is $25. Would you like a **receipt**?
* **Role A:** Yes, please. Thank you!

## 7. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | When to use it (Context) |
| --- | --- |
| **Excuse me...** | To get someone's attention politely before asking a question. |
| **Actually...** | To introduce a specific request or clarify what you want. |
| **Could I... / Would you happen to have...?** | To make a request more polite and less direct. |
| **To be honest...** | To share a personal opinion or preference. |
| **I’m afraid...** | To politely introduce a negative answer or a problem. |
| **I think I'll take it.** | A polite way to confirm you want to buy an item. |
