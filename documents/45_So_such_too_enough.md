# 45. So, such, too, enough

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Too** | Used to say that there is more than enough of something, often with a negative meaning. | Занадто |
| **Enough** | Used to say that there is as much as is needed or wanted. | Достатньо |
| **So** | Used before an adjective or adverb to mean "very" or "to such a high degree". | Так / Настільки |
| **Such** | Used before an adjective and a noun to emphasize a quality. | Такий / Настільки |
| **To reach** | To be able to stretch your arm and touch or grab something. | Дістати / Досягнути |
| **Qualified** | Having the necessary skills, knowledge, or qualifications for a job. | Кваліфікований |
| **Promotion** | A move to a more important job or rank in a company. | Підвищення |
| **Mature** | Behaving in a sensible way, like an adult. | Зрілий / Дорослий |
| **Yacht** | A large, expensive boat used for racing or pleasure. | Яхта |
| **Recipe** | A set of instructions for cooking a particular type of food. | Рецепт |

## 2. Vocabulary Hooks

> To remember the difference: **So** is "so" simple (just one word follows), but **Such** needs a "such" substantial partner (a noun phrase).
> If you have **too** much of a good thing, it becomes a bad thing (negative opinion).

## 3. Grammar Essentials

### Too
Formula: `Too + Adjective / Adverb`
Formula: `Too much / many + Noun`
Вживається для вираження надмірності, що зазвичай має негативний відтінок.
*Example: It is too hot to go outside.*
*Example: James speaks too quietly.*
*Example: I ate too many sandwiches.*

### Enough
Formula: `Adjective / Adverb + Enough`
Formula: `Enough + Noun`
Вживається для позначення достатньої кількості або міри чогось.
*Example: He is old enough to drive.*
*Example: We have enough chairs.*
*Example: Are you warm enough?*

### So vs Such
Formula: `So + Adjective / Adverb`
Formula: `Such + (a/an) + Adjective + Noun`
Обидва слова підсилюють значення "дуже", але мають різну структуру в реченні.
*Example: She is so tall.*
*Example: It is such a beautiful day.*
*Example: That's such a pretty dress!*

### Result Clauses (So/Such... that)
Formula: `So/Such... + that + Result`
Використовується для опису причини та її наслідку.
*Example: She felt so upset that she started to cry.*
*Example: They had such an awful time that they said they’d never go again.*

## 4. Written Practice

### Fill in the blanks:
1. The soup is **too** hot to eat.
2. He is **such** a talented artist.
3. The weather is **so** beautiful today.
4. She didn’t run fast **enough** to win the race.
5. There are **so/too** many people in the room.
6. She has **too** many books to read.
7. The movie was **so** interesting that I watched it twice.
8. He is not tall **enough** to reach the top shelf.
9. They are **such** good friends.
10. The exam was **so** difficult that many students failed.
11. She is **too** young to go to the party alone.
12. Do we have **enough** time to finish the project?
13. It was **such** a great party that we stayed all night.
14. The coffee is **too** strong for my taste.
15. He worked **so** hard that he got a promotion.

### Sentence Building (Combined):
1. He is very short. He can’t be a basketball player. -> **He is too short to be a basketball player.**
2. She has saved enough money. She can go on holiday. -> **She has saved enough money to go on holiday.**
3. This tea is very hot. I can’t drink it. -> **This tea is too hot to drink.**
4. Daisy is very young. She can’t go to school. -> **Daisy is too young to go to school.**
5. Paul is rich. He can buy a yacht. -> **Paul is rich enough to buy a yacht.**
6. The cake was very delicious. Everyone asked for the recipe. -> **The cake was so delicious that everyone asked for the recipe.**
7. This flat is small. We can’t live in it any more. -> **This flat is too small for us to live in anymore.**
8. She is a kind person. Everyone likes her. -> **She is such a kind person that everyone likes her.**

### Translation Block
- Це настільки цікава книга, що я не можу її закрити. -> **It is such an interesting book that I can't put it down.**
- Ти достатньо дорослий, щоб приймати власні рішення. -> **You are old enough to make your own decisions.**
- Сьогодні занадто холодно, щоб іти на прогулянку. -> **It is too cold to go for a walk today.**

## 5. Speaking & Discussion Guide

**Q: Have you ever been too tired to finish a task? What did you do?**
* **Positive/Productive:** "To be honest, I have felt that way, so I decided to take a short nap. After resting, I actually found it much easier to complete the project."
* **Neutral:** "Actually, it happens occasionally. I usually just try to push through it with a cup of coffee, though it might not be the best solution."
* **Negative:** "I'm afraid I have. I was so exhausted that I simply had to leave the task for the next morning because I couldn't focus at all."

**Q: Do you think you get enough sleep every night? Why or why not?**
* **Positive:** "I'd say I generally get enough rest. I try to maintain a strict schedule because I'm much more productive when I'm well-rested."
* **Neutral:** "Perhaps I get enough on weekends, but during the week, I might stay up a bit too late working on my assignments."
* **Negative:** "To be honest, I rarely get enough sleep. I'm often too busy with various responsibilities to go to bed at a reasonable hour."

**Q: What is something that makes you so happy? Why does it make you happy?**
* **Positive:** "Spending time with my family makes me so happy because they are such supportive people. It's always a wonderful experience."
* **Neutral:** "Actually, finishing a difficult task gives me a sense of satisfaction. It might not be 'pure' happiness, but it feels good to be productive."
* **Negative:** "I'm afraid I don't have much time for hobbies lately, but traveling used to make me very happy as it was such an eye-opening experience."

**Q: Can you remember a time when you had such a good time that you didn’t want it to end?**
* **Positive:** "I remember a summer trip with friends. We were having such a wonderful time exploring the city that we completely lost track of time."
* **Neutral:** "Perhaps during my last birthday party. It was such a pleasant evening with colleagues that I wished we could stay longer."
* **Negative:** "To be honest, it's been a while since I had such an experience. Life has been a bit too hectic recently to fully enjoy the moment."

## 6. Professional Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Rejecting a Proposal (Polite English)**
*Context: A manager explains why a certain software is not suitable for the team.*
**Role A (Manager):** "I’ve reviewed the new software. To be honest, I’m afraid it might be **too complicated** for our current needs."
**Role B (Employee):** "I see. Do you think our team isn't **experienced enough** to use it efficiently yet?"
**Role A (Manager):** "Exactly. It's **such a powerful tool**, but perhaps we should look for something a bit more user-friendly for now."

**Scenario 2: Job Interview Feedback**
*Context: A recruiter provides feedback to a candidate.*
**Role A (Recruiter):** "Your portfolio is **so impressive** that our CEO personally requested a second interview."
**Role B (Candidate):** "That's very kind of you to say. I hope my experience is **broad enough** to contribute to your upcoming project."
**Role A (Recruiter):** "Actually, you are **such a strong candidate** that we are considering you for a senior position."

## 7. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | When to use it (Context) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **To be honest / Honestly** | Use when sharing a potentially disappointing opinion or truth. |
| **I’m afraid (that)...** | Use to introduce bad news or a disagreement politely. |
| **Actually** | Use to correct someone or clarify a point without being aggressive. |
| **Perhaps / Maybe** | Use to make a suggestion or criticism sound less direct and softer. |
| **I'd say / I would say** | Use to express an opinion in a less "final" or bossy way. |
| **Could / Might / Would** | Use these modal verbs to make requests or statements sound more tentative and polite. |
