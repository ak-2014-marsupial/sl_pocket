## 2. Grammar Essentials: Conditional Sentences

### Zero Conditional (Facts and Truths)

* **Formula:** `If + Present Simple, Present Simple`
* **Usage:** Used for general truths or scientific facts.
* **Example:** If I heat ice cream, it melts.

### First Conditional (Real Possibilities)

* **Formula:** `If + Present Simple, Will + Verb`
* **Usage:** Used for real and possible situations in the future.
* **Example:** If I have time, I will watch a movie tonight.

### Second Conditional (Imaginary Situations)

* **Formula:** `If + Past Simple, Would + Verb`
* **Usage:** Used for imaginary, unlikely, or impossible situations in the present or future.
* **Important Note:** In formal English, we often use **"were"** for all subjects (If I *were* you, If he *were* rich).
* **Example:** If I won the lottery, I would travel the world.

## 3. Written Practice (Completed Exercises)

**Exercise: Filling the gaps & Sentence Completion**

1. If I **could**, I **would go** to the movies.
2. If I **had** a friend, I **would not feel** lonely.
3. If she **were** a veterinarian, she **would treat** animals.
4. If we **had** a map, we **would find** the way.
5. If I **received** a gift, I **would be** very happy.

**Translation Block (UA to EN):**

* *Якби я міг, я б пішов у кіно.* — If I could, I would go to the cinema.
* *Якби у мене був друг, я б не почувався самотнім.* — If I had a friend, I would not feel lonely.
* *Якби вона була ветеринаром, вона б лікувала тварин.* — If she were a veterinarian, she would treat animals.
* *Якби ми мали мапу, ми б знайшли дорогу.* — If we had a map, we would find the way.
* *Якби я отримав подарунок, я був би дуже щасливий.* — If I received a gift, I would be absolutely delighted.

## 4. Speaking & Discussion Guide

### Discussion Questions & Sample Answers

**Q1: If you found a bag of cash on the street, what would you do?**

* **Positive/Honest:** "To be honest, I would probably try to find the owner first by taking it to the police, as I believe in doing the right thing."
* **Negative/Self-focused:** "Actually, if I'm being realistic, I might keep a small part of it for a rainy day before reporting it, although I know that's not ideal."
* **Neutral/Diplomatic:** "I’m afraid it’s a difficult dilemma. I could potentially ask around the nearby shops to see if anyone lost something valuable."

**Q2: If you won the lottery, how would your life change?**

* **Positive:** "It would be wonderful! I would most likely invest in a new business and perhaps donate a portion to charity."
* **Negative:** "Actually, I'm afraid it might make life more complicated because many people would suddenly want to be my friends for the wrong reasons."
* **Neutral:** "Well, to be fair, I don't think I would change much. I would probably just buy a more comfortable house and continue my studies."

**Q3: If you could be invisible for one day, where would you go?**

* **Creative:** "I might consider visiting some high-level government meetings just to see how big decisions are made behind closed doors."
* **Candid:** "Actually, I would probably just go to a famous concert and stand right on the stage to experience the atmosphere."

## 5. Professional Scenarios (Roleplay)

**Scenario: Career Change Advice**
*Context: A colleague is considering switching careers but is afraid.*

* **Person A:** "I've been thinking... if I had more courage, I would quit my job and start a career in IT."
* **Person B:** "Actually, I think you're very talented. If you *were* to start a course now, you *could* be ready for junior roles in six months. Would you consider looking at some bootcamps?"
* **Person A:** "I might. To be honest, the financial side is what worries me the most."

## 6. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase/Softener | How to use it |
| --- | --- |
| **Actually...** | Used to correct someone gently or introduce a different opinion. |
| **To be honest / To be fair...** | Used when you are about to say something that might be surprising or slightly negative. |
| **I'm afraid...** | Used to politely introduce bad news or a disagreement (e.g., "I'm afraid I don't agree"). |
| **Perhaps / Maybe...** | Used to make your suggestions sound less bossy. |
| **I might / I could...** | Using these instead of "I will" makes your plans sound more thoughtful and less aggressive. |
| **Would you consider...?** | A very polite way to give advice instead of saying "You should...". |
