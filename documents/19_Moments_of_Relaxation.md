## 3. Grammar Essentials

**Expressing Interests and Preferences**

**Formula 1: Positive Interest**
`Subject + be (am/is/are) + interested in + Gerund/Noun`
Вживається для опису речей або занять, які нам подобаються або якими ми цікавимося.
*Example: I am interested in travelling and trying new recipes.*

**Formula 2: Dislike / Lack of Interest**
`Subject + be (am/is/are) + not + interested in + Gerund/Noun`
Вживається для опису того, що нам не до вподоби.
*Example: She isn't interested in hiking at all.*

**Formula 3: Informal Preference**
`Noun/Gerund + be (is/are) + not my thing`
Ввічливий та неформальний спосіб сказати, що певне заняття вам не підходить або не подобається ("це не моє").
*Example: To be honest, martial arts are not my thing.*

## 5. Speaking & Discussion Guide

**Q: Do you prefer spending your free time alone or with friends? Why?**
* **Positive:** I actually prefer spending time with friends because it gives me a lot of positive emotions and we always have fun together.
* **Neutral:** To be honest, it might depend on my mood; sometimes I enjoy hanging out with friends, but other times I need some peace and quiet alone.
* **Negative:** I’m afraid I find socializing a bit exhausting, so I usually prefer staying home and working on my hobbies alone.

**Q: Have you ever tried yoga? If so, how did it make you feel?**
* **Positive:** Yes, I’ve tried it! It made me feel incredibly relaxed and helped me focus on my breathing.
* **Neutral:** I did try it once, though it was a bit challenging for me. I could say it was an interesting experience, but I haven't done it since.
* **Negative:** Actually, yoga is not really my thing. I tried a class, but I found it a bit too slow and preferred more active sports.

**Q: Is there a hobby you’ve always wanted to try but haven’t yet?**
* **Positive:** I’ve always been interested in martial arts. It looks like a great way to stay fit and learn self-defense.
* **Neutral:** I might consider trying hiking one day if I have enough free time, though I’m not in a rush to start.
* **Negative:** To be honest, I'm quite happy with my current routine, so there isn't anything specific I’m looking to try at the moment.

## 6. Professional Scenarios

**Context:** Two colleagues are discussing their plans for the upcoming weekend during a coffee break.

**Scenario:**
* **Role A:** I was wondering if you have any exciting plans for the weekend?
* **Role B:** Well, I’m actually planning to go hiking in the mountains. I find it very relaxing. Would you be interested in joining?
* **Role A:** That sounds lovely, but I’m afraid hiking isn’t really my thing. I’m more of a "stay-at-home" person. I’ll probably just try a new recipe and play some board games with my family.
* **Role B:** I completely understand! Everyone has their own way of relaxing. Enjoy your cooking!

## 7. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | When to use it (Context) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **To be honest...** | Use this to introduce an opinion that might be different from others or to say "no" politely. |
| **I'm afraid...** | A very polite way to introduce bad news or a disagreement (e.g., declining an invitation). |
| **Actually...** | Used to correct someone gently or to give more specific information about your preferences. |
| **...isn't my thing** | A soft and indirect way to say you don't like something without being rude. |
| **I was wondering if...** | A polite way to start a question or make a suggestion. |
| **Could / Would / Might** | Modal verbs used to make requests or statements sound less direct and more professional. |
