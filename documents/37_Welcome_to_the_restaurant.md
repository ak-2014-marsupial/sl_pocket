# Welcome to the restaurant!

### **1. Vocabulary & Definitions**

| Word / Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **A waiter / A waitress** | A person who brings food and drinks to people. | Офіціант / офіціантка |
| **A reservation** | A request to keep a table for you at a restaurant. | Бронювання |
| **To book a table** | To ask a restaurant to save a table for you. | Забронювати столик |
| **To be fully booked** | All tables are taken; there are no free spots. | Все зайнято / немає вільних місць |
| **Bill / Check** | A piece of paper that shows how much you have to pay. | Рахунок |
| **Same for me** | Used to order the same thing as someone else. | Мені теж саме |
| **Service** | The way you are treated by the staff. | Обслуговування |
| **Order** | A request for food or drinks. | Замовлення |

---

### **2. Grammar Essentials**

* **Future Intention (Ordering):** * *Formula:* **I will have + [dish/drink].**
 
*Example:* "I will have the chicken pasta, please." 

* **Polite Requests:** * *Formula:* **Can we get / Can I have + [item], please?**
 
*Example:* "Can we get the bill, please?" 


* **Complaints (Polite):** * *Formula:* **I am sorry, but / Excuse me, + [problem].**
 
*Example:* "I am sorry, but my food is too cold."

### **3. Written Practice (Completed Exercises)**

* **Waiter:** Welcome to the restaurant! How many are in your party?
* **Customer:** We have four people with us today.
 
**Waiter:** Do you **have a reservation**? 

**Customer:** Yes, I made a reservation for 7 PM. 

**Waiter:** We are **fully booked** for tonight. 

* **Customer:** Is there a chance for a table later?

**Ordering Scenarios:**

**Waiter:** What **can I get you**? 


* **Customer:** I would like a salad and a lemonade, please.
* **Customer 1:** I will have the chicken pasta.
 
**Customer 2:** **Same for** me, please. 

**Handling Issues:**

**Customer:** **I am sorry but** my food is too cold. 

**Waiter:** Let me take it back and warm it up for you. 

**Customer:** **Excuse me, this** is not what I ordered. 

**Customer:** **Can we get** the bill, please?

### **4. Speaking & Discussion Guide**

Q1: Do you prefer to eat at a restaurant or cook at home? Why? 

* **Positive (Restaurant):** "To be honest, I prefer eating out because I really enjoy trying different cuisines that I can't cook myself. It’s also a lovely way to relax without worrying about the washing up!"
* **Negative (Home):** "Actually, I'd much rather cook at home. I find it quite therapeutic, and it's usually much healthier and more cost-effective than dining at a restaurant."
* **Neutral:** "It depends, really. I like the convenience of a restaurant on weekends, but during the week, I prefer a home-cooked meal."

**Q2: What is your favorite type of restaurant?** 

* **Option 1:** "I'm quite fond of Italian restaurants. Could there be anything better than a fresh pasta or a wood-fired pizza?"
* **Option 2:** "I’m afraid I’m a bit boring, but I actually love Japanese food. The presentation is always so beautiful and the flavors are very delicate."

**Q3: How do you feel about service in restaurants in your country?** 

* **Response:** "Generally speaking, I think the service is quite good. However, sometimes it can be a bit slow during peak hours. I would say it’s improving every year."

### **5. Professional Dialogue Scenarios**

**Scenario: A Difficult Arrival (No Reservation)** 

* **Waiter:** Good evening! Welcome to 'The Green Bistro'. Do you have a reservation?
* **Customer:** Good evening. I'm afraid we haven't booked a table. Is there any chance you could fit us in?
* **Waiter:** I’m terribly sorry, but we are actually fully booked for tonight. However, if you could wait for about 20 minutes, we might have a table free.
* **Customer:** That would be wonderful, thank you for checking.

**Scenario: The Wrong Order** 

* **Customer:** Excuse me, I'm sorry to bother you, but I think there's been a slight mistake. I ordered the steak, but I've been brought the fish instead.
* **Waiter:** Oh, I am so sorry about that! Let me take this back to the kitchen immediately and get your steak for you.
* **Customer:** That’s quite alright, thank you.

### **6. Polite English Cheat Sheet**

* **Softeners (Пом’якшувачі):**
* *Actually...* (Використовуйте, коли хочете делікатно заперечити або уточнити).
* *To be honest...* (Для висловлення особистої думки).
* *I'm afraid...* (Коли ви змушені повідомити погані новини, наприклад: "Боюсь, у нас немає місць").
* *I'm sorry to bother you, but...* (Ідеально для початку скарги).


* **Modal Verbs (Модальні дієслова):**
* *Could we... / Would you mind...* (Для дуже ввічливих прохань).
* *I would like...* (Замість грубого "I want").


* **Indirect Questions (Непрямі запитання):**
* *Is there any chance...?* (Замість "Can I...?").
* *I was wondering if...?* (Дуже ввічливий спосіб щось запитати).
