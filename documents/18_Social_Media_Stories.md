# 18. Social Media Stories

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Keep in touch with somebody** |  To maintain communications with someone. | Підтримувати зв'язок з кимось |
| **Scroll through social media** |  To move through the content on a social media platform. | Гортати соціальні мережі |
| **Check messages** |  To look at and read new communications received. | Перевіряти повідомлення |
| **Share photos and videos** |  To post or send visual content to others. | Ділитись фото та відео |
| **Tag a friend** |  To mention someone in a post or photo so they are notified. | Відмітити друга |
| **Follow/Unfollow somebody** |  To subscribe to or unsubscribe from someone's updates. | Підписатись на/відписатись від когось |
| **Go viral** |  To become extremely popular very quickly on the internet. | Стати вірусним (дуже популярним) |
| **Newsfeed** |  A continuous update of content from sources you follow. | Стрічка новин |
| **Notifications** |  Alerts or messages that inform you of new activity. | Сповіщення |
| **Trending** |  Currently popular or widely discussed online. | В тренді (популярний) |
| **Influencer** |  A person with the power to affect the decisions of others online. | Лідер думок / інфлюенсер |

## 2. Vocabulary Hooks

>  To **scroll** is like a stroll through a digital park of news.
>  When something **goes viral**, it spreads faster than a cold in winter.
>  Use **notifications** so you never miss a "notion" of what's happening.

## 3. Grammar Essentials

**Formula:** `Subject + Verb (Present Simple) + Object`
 Present Simple is used to describe regular habits and routines on social media.

*  *Example: She **checks** her notifications every morning.*
*  *Example: I **scroll** through social media before going to bed.*

## 4. Written Practice

1.   I love to **share** photos and videos from my birthday party.
2.   When I want to see what’s new, I scroll through my **newsfeed**.
3.   She always **checks** her messages right after waking up.
4.   My favorite singer is **trending** on Instagram right now.
5.   I **tagged** a friend in my Instagram post from the concert.
6.   The video of the puppy **went viral** and got millions of views.
7.   To stay connected with my cousin who lives abroad, I **keep in touch with** her on WhatsApp.
8.   I get too many **notifications** from social media apps on my phone.
9.   He decided to **unfollow** that YouTube channel because he didn’t like the content.
10.  My friend became **an influencer** on TikTok by sharing makeup tutorials.

### Translation Block
*  Залишатись на зв’язку з кимось — **To keep in touch with somebody**.
*  Гортати соціальні мережі — **To scroll through social media**.
*  Стати дуже популярним — **To go viral**.

## 5. Speaking & Discussion Guide

 **Q: How often do you scroll through social media each day, and what do you usually look for? **
* **Positive:** To be honest, I enjoy scrolling through my newsfeed for about an hour daily because I find the educational content quite inspiring.
* **Neutral:** I suppose I check my social media a few times a day, mostly just to see what’s trending or if there are any new updates from friends.
* **Negative:** I’m afraid I spend more time than I should scrolling; it can be a bit distracting when I’m trying to focus on work.

 **Q: Do you think notifications are helpful or distracting? **
* **Positive:** I actually find them very helpful because they allow me to keep in touch with my colleagues and respond to messages promptly.
* **Neutral:** It might depend on the app; some notifications are useful, while others just feel like unnecessary noise.
* **Negative:** I personally find them quite distracting, so I usually turn them off to stay focused on my tasks.

## 6. Professional Scenarios

**Context:** Discussing a viral marketing campaign at the office.
**Role A:** "Could you please tell me if our latest video has **gone viral** yet?"
**Role B:** "Actually, it is currently **trending** on TikTok, and we are receiving a lot of **notifications** from new followers."

**Context:** Chatting about staying connected with international clients.
**Role A:** "How do you usually **keep in touch with** our partners who live far away?"
**Role B:** "I'm afraid I mostly use LinkedIn to **check messages** and see their latest updates in the **newsfeed**."

## 7. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | When to use it (Context) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Actually** | Use this to gently correct someone or add a contrasting fact. |
| **To be honest** | Use this when sharing a personal opinion or a slightly negative truth. |
| **I’m afraid** | Use this to politely introduce bad news or a disagreement. |
| **Could you / Would you** | Use these modal verbs to make requests sound more professional and less demanding. |
| **I suppose** | Use this when you are not 100% certain or want to sound less aggressive in your opinion. |
