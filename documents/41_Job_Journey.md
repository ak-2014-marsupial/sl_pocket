#  41. Job Journey

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions
| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Make money** |  To earn money from a job or business. | Заробляти гроші |
| **Full-time job** |  A job that occupies the whole of a person's working week (usually 40 hours).  | Робота з повною зайнятістю |
| **Part-time job** |  A job that occupies only part of a person's working week.  | Робота з частковою зайнятістю |
| **Accountant** |  A person who works with money, numbers, and checks financial records.  | Бухгалтер |
| **Engineer** |  A person who designs and builds machines, roads, or bridges.  | Інженер |
| **Lawyer** |  A person who helps people with legal problems and the law.  | Юрист / Адвокат |
| **Sales manager** |  A person who leads a sales team and sells products to customers.  | Менеджер з продажу |
| **Software developer** |  A person who writes and creates computer programs.  | Розробник програмного забезпечення |
| **Graphic designer** |  A person who creates visual designs, posters, and logos.  | Графічний дизайнер |
| **Scientist** |  A person who studies science to discover new things in a laboratory.  | Науковець |
| **Politician** |  A person who works in government and is active in politics.  | Політик |
| **Start a business** |  To begin a new commercial organization or company.  | Заснувати бізнес |
| **Take courses** |  To participate in a series of lessons to learn new skills.  | Проходити курси |
| **Work long hours** |  To work for a period of time that is longer than the usual working day.  | Працювати понаднормово |

## 2. Vocabulary Hooks
>  I **make money** to **start a business** that helps people. 
>  An **accountant** counts every cent to ensure the **full-time** staff is paid correctly. 
>  The **software developer** writes code while the **graphic designer** creates the logo. 

## 3. Grammar Essentials
**Formula: [Subject] + [Verb (s/es)] + [Object]**
 Ця формула використовується для опису професійних обов'язків у Present Simple, що є актуальним для постійних дій або фактів про роботу. 

*  *Example: An engineer designs machines.* 
*  *Example: A software developer writes computer programs.* 
*  *Example: A lawyer helps people solve legal problems.* 

## 4. Written Practice
 I want to be an **accountant** (1) when I grow up, I like numbers.   My brother works as an **engineer** (2), and he designs bridges and buildings.   My sister is a **lawyer** (3); she helps people with legal problems.   My best friend wants to be a **software developer** (4) because he loves creating computer programs.   Some people prefer to start **a business** (5), while others look for **part-time** (6) jobs with flexible hours.   Personally, I hope to take **courses** (7) to learn new skills and prepare for my career. 

### Translation Block
1. Він графічний дизайнер; він створює красиві плакати.
    **He is a graphic designer; he creates beautiful posters.** 
2. Моя сестра хоче заснувати бізнес наступного року.
    **My sister wants to start a business next year.** 
3. Науковці роблять відкриття в лабораторії.
    **Scientists discover new things in the laboratory.** 

## 5. Speaking & Discussion Guide
 **Q: What job did you want to have as a child?** 
*  **Positive:** To be honest, I've always dreamed of being a scientist because I was quite curious about how the world works. 
*  **Neutral:** Actually, I haven't thought about it much, but I suppose being an engineer seemed interesting at the time. 
* **Negative:** I'm afraid I didn't have a specific dream job, as I was more focused on my hobbies rather than a professional career.

**Q: Do you prefer a full-time job or a part-time job?  Why?** 
*  **Positive:** I personally prefer a full-time job because it provides a stable income and a clear structure to my day. 
*  **Neutral:** To be fair, both have their merits; a part-time job offers more flexibility, which I might find useful. 
*  **Negative:** I'm afraid I don't enjoy the lack of benefits in part-time roles, but full-time work can sometimes feel quite exhausting. 

**Q: Would you like to start your own business?  What kind of business?** 
*  **Positive:** I would certainly love to start a software development company, as I'm quite passionate about technology. 
*  **Neutral:** I might consider starting a business in the future, though I’m currently focused on improving my professional skills.
* **Negative:** To be honest, I'm not sure if I'm ready for the risks involved in starting a business at this stage.

**Q: Do you think working long hours is good or bad?  Why?** 
*  **Positive:** Sometimes working long hours is necessary to achieve important goals and meet deadlines.
* **Neutral:** It could be acceptable occasionally, but I believe maintaining a work-life balance is also quite important.
*  **Negative:** I'm afraid working long hours is generally bad because it can lead to burnout and decreased productivity.

## 6. Professional Scenarios
**Context: Career Counseling**
Role A: To be honest, I'm looking for a career change.  Could you suggest a role for someone who likes numbers? 
Role B: Actually, you might enjoy being an accountant.  It involves working with money and checking financial records. 

**Context: Networking Event**
Role A: I noticed you work in the tech industry.  What exactly do you do? 
Role B: I'm a software developer.  I write computer programs and help start-up businesses with their technical needs. 

**Context: Job Interview**
 Role A: Could you tell me if you are looking for a full-time or part-time position? 
 Role B: I’m actually looking for a full-time job, but I’m open to working long hours if the project is interesting. 

## 7. Polite English Cheat Sheet
| Phrase / Softener | When to use it (Context) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Actually** | Use this to correct someone politely or add a surprising fact. |
| **To be honest** | Use this when giving a personal opinion that might be unexpected. |
| **I'm afraid** |  Use this to introduce bad news or a disagreement politely.  |
| **Could / Would / Might** |  Use these modal verbs to make requests or suggestions sound softer.  |
| **I suppose** |  Use this when you are not 100% sure but want to provide an answer.  |
