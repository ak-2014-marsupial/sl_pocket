# 13. Shop Smart

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **to shop online** | To buy goods or services over the internet. | купувати онлайн |
| **to spend** | To give money to pay for goods or services. | витрачати |
| **to go shopping** | The activity of visiting shops and buying things. | ходити за покупками |
| **a price** | The amount of money that you have to pay for something. | ціна |
| **a discount** | A reduction in the usual price of something. | знижка |
| **to pay** | To give someone money for something you have bought. | платити |
| **expensive** | Costing a lot of money. | дорогий |
| **cheap** | Not costing a lot of money. | дешевий |
| **a shopping list** | A list of things that you need to buy when you go shopping. | список покупок |
| **a shop window** | A large window at the front of a shop where goods are displayed. | вітрина магазину |


## 3. Grammar Essentials

**Formula: Subject + Verb (spend) + Money + on + Noun** 

В англійській мові ми використовуємо прийменник **on** після дієслова **spend**, коли говоримо про те, на що саме витрачаємо гроші.

Example: She spends a lot of money on books and travel.
Example: I don’t like to spend too much money on things I don’t need.

**Formula: to be + expensive / cheap** 

Використовуйте дієслово-зв'язку (am/is/are), щоб описати вартість предмета. "Too" перед прикметником підсилює значення "надто".

Example: This bag is too expensive for me.
Example: I bought this T-shirt because it was cheap.

## 4. Written Practice

1. I like to **go** shopping with my friends on weekends.

2. He decided to **shop** online for a new phone.

3. She checked the **price** of the shoes before buying them.

4. The store is offering a big **discount** on all summer clothes.

5. We wrote down everything we need on the **shopping list**.

6. The **shop window** showed a new collection of bags.

7. This store is known for its **cheap** clothes.

8. I don’t like to **spend** too much money on things I don’t need.

9. She paid a very high **price** for that designer handbag.


**Translation Block**

* Вона витрачає багато грошей на подорожі. — She spends a lot of money on travel.

* Ця сукня занадто дорога для мене. — This dress is too expensive for me.

* Я завжди складаю список покупок перед походом до супермаркету. — I always make a shopping list before going to the supermarket.



## 5. Speaking & Discussion Guide

Q: How often do you go shopping, and what do you usually buy? 

* **Positive:** To be honest, I go shopping quite often, maybe once a week. I usually buy fresh groceries and occasionally some items for my home.
* **Neutral:** I go shopping when I actually need something specific. Most of the time, I just stick to my basic necessities like food and household supplies.
* **Negative:** I'm afraid I don't go shopping very often because I find it quite tiring. I only go when it's absolutely necessary to restock my kitchen.

Q: Do you prefer to shop online or in stores? Why? 

* **Positive:** I definitely prefer shopping online. It might be more convenient since I can compare prices easily and it really saves a lot of time.
* **Neutral:** Actually, it depends on the item. I shop online for electronics, but I prefer physical stores for clothes so I can try them on.
* **Negative:** To be honest, I'm not a big fan of online shopping. I'm afraid that the quality might not be what I expect, so I prefer seeing things in person.

Q: What’s the most expensive item you’ve ever bought? Was it worth the price? 

* **Positive:** The most expensive thing I’ve bought was my laptop. It was quite a large spend, but it was definitely worth the price for my work.
* **Neutral:** I once bought a designer watch. It was a bit expensive, though I suppose it's worth it because the quality is very high.

## 6. Professional Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Asking about a discount** 
*Context: A customer is looking at a pair of boots in a store.*
**Role A (Customer):** Excuse me, could you tell me if these boots are on sale? 

* **Role B (Shop Assistant):** Yes, they are! Actually, these boots are 30 percent off right now.

* **Role A (Customer):** Amazing! That is not too expensive at all.

**Scenario 2: Planning a shopping trip** 
*Context: Two friends are discussing their weekend plans.*
**Role A:** I need to make a shopping list before I go to the store.
* **Role B:** What do you need to buy? I don't want to spend much money today.
* **Role A:** Just some groceries. Would you like to go to the mall or shop online? 


## 7. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | When to use it (Context) |
| --- | --- |
| **Actually** | Used to correct someone gently or add a surprising fact. |
| **To be honest** | Used when expressing a personal opinion or a slight negative. |
| **I'm afraid** | Used to politely introduce bad news or a disagreement. |
| **Could you... / Would you...** | Used to make requests more polite and less demanding. |
| **Quite / A bit** | Used to soften strong adjectives like "expensive." |
