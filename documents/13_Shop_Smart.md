# Shop Smart

### 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **To shop online** | To buy goods or services over the internet. | Купувати онлайн |
| **To spend** | To give money to pay for goods or services.  | Витрачати |
| **To go shopping** | The activity of visiting shops to buy things. | Ходити за покупками |
| **A price** | The amount of money that you have to pay for something. | Ціна |
| **A discount** | A reduction in the usual price of something.  | Знижка |
| **To pay** | To give money for something that you buy or for a service. | Платити |
| **Expensive** | Costing a lot of money; not cheap.  | Дорогий |
| **Cheap** | Costing little money or less than is usual or expected. | Дешевий |
| **A shopping list** | A list of things that you want to buy when you go to a shop. | Список покупок |
| **A shop window** | A large window at the front of a shop where things for sale are displayed. | Вітрина магазину |

### 2. Grammar Essentials


* **Spend money ON something:** Ми використовуємо прийменник **on**, коли говоримо про витрати на певні товари чи послуги.
 
*Example:* She spends a lot of money **on** clothes. 


* **Pay FOR something:** Завжди використовуйте **for**, коли вказуєте об'єкт оплати.
 
*Example:* Don't forget to pay **for** the bread. 


* **Too + Adjective:** Використовується для позначення надмірності (більше, ніж потрібно).
 
*Example:* This bag is **too expensive** for me.

### 4. Speaking & Discussion Guide

**Questions and Model Answers (Polite Style):**

1. 
**How often do you go shopping, and what do you usually buy?** 


* *Positive:* "To be honest, I go shopping quite often, usually once a week. I mostly look for fresh groceries and occasionally some household items."
* *Neutral:* "Actually, I don't go shopping very frequently. I try to visit a supermarket once every two weeks to stock up on essentials."

2. Do you prefer to shop online or in stores? Why? 

* *Preference for Online:* "I personally find it much more convenient to shop online. It saves a lot of time and, to be fair, the prices are often more competitive."
* *Preference for Stores:* "I'm afraid I still prefer physical stores. I like to see the quality of products firsthand before I decide to pay for them."

3. What’s the most expensive item you’ve ever bought? Was it worth the price? 

* *Positive:* "Actually, the most expensive thing I've bought was my laptop. It was quite a big investment, but it has definitely been worth the price for my studies."
* *Negative:* "To be honest, I once bought a designer watch. It was very expensive, but I'm afraid it wasn't really worth it, as I rarely wear it."


4. Do you usually compare prices before making a purchase? How do you do it? 

* *Method:* "I usually compare prices online before buying anything significant. I might check several websites to see if there is a discount available."


**Scenario: Asking about the Price (Adapted from slides)** 

* **Situation:** George is at the store but can't find a price tag.
* **Polite Dialogue:**
* **George:** "Excuse me, could you possibly help me? I’m interested in this shirt, but I can’t seem to find the price."
* **Shop Assistant:** "Of course! Let me check that for you. It’s currently 25 dollars."
* **George:** "Oh, I see. Would there happen to be any discount on this item today?"

### 5. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | How and when to use |
| --- | --- |
| **Actually / To be honest** | Used to introduce a personal opinion or a fact that might be unexpected. |
| **I’m afraid...** | Used to politely introduce bad news or a disagreement (e.g., "I'm afraid it's too expensive"). |
| **Could you...? / Would you...?** | Modal verbs that make a request sound much softer than a direct command. |
| **I was wondering if...** | A very polite way to ask for information or a favor. |
| **Quite / A bit** | Used to soften an adjective (e.g., "It's a bit expensive" sounds better than "It's expensive"). |
| **Would there happen to be...?** | A very indirect way to ask if something exists (like a discount). |
