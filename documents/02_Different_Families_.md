## 2. Grammar Essentials: Collective Nouns (Family)

Коли ми говоримо про слово **"family"**, використання дієслова залежить від контексту та варіанту англійської мови:

* **Singular (is):** Використовується, коли ми розглядаємо сім'ю як єдине ціле (Unit). Це типово для американської англійської.

*Example:* My family **is** planning a surprise party.

* **Plural (are):** Використовується, коли ми маємо на увазі окремих членів сім'ї. Це частіше зустрічається в британській англійській.

*Example:* The Johnson family **are** very talented.

## 4. Speaking & Discussion Guide

Q1: How many siblings do you have? What do you enjoy doing together? 

* *Positive:* To be honest, I’m quite lucky to have two siblings. We really enjoy going on hiking trips whenever we have some free time.
* *Negative:* Actually, I don’t have any siblings; I’m an only child. However, I often spend time with my cousins, and we quite enjoy watching movies together.
* *Neutral:* I have one brother. We don't see each other very often, but we might grab a coffee once a month to catch up.

Q2: Who is the oldest and youngest member in your family? 

* *Detailed response:* I’m afraid I don't know the exact ages of everyone, but my grandfather is definitely the oldest member, being 85. On the other hand, my niece is the youngest; she’s just a toddler.

Q3: How do you get along with your mother-in-law or father-in-law? 

* *Polite response:* Generally speaking, I get along quite well with my mother-in-law. We occasionally enjoy cooking together, which I find very pleasant. To be fair, my father-in-law is a bit more reserved, but we still have great conversations about history.

Q4: Do you have a favorite family memory? 

* *Detailed response:* Actually, one of my favorite memories is our trip to the seaside five years ago. It was special because it was the last time the whole family could gather together before my brother moved abroad.

## 5. Scenario: Professional Dialogue Practice

*Ситуація: Колега запитує вас про плани на відпустку та вашу родину.*

**Colleague:** "Are you planning to go anywhere special for the holidays?"

**You:** "**Actually**, I’m planning a small family reunion. My **spouse** and I would like to host a dinner for my **mother-in-law** and a few other **family members**."

**Colleague:** "That sounds lovely! Do you have a big family?"

**You:** "**To be honest**, it’s not very large. I’m an **only child**, but my spouse has several **siblings**, so our **nephews** and **nieces** usually make the house quite lively!"

## 6. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | How to use it |
| --- | --- |
| **Actually / To be honest** | Використовуйте, щоб ввічливо виправити когось або висловити думку, яка може здивувати.|
| **I’m afraid...** | Використовується для ввічливого повідомлення про те, що ви чогось не знаєте або не можете зробити. |
| **Would / Could / Might** | Модальні дієслова для пом'якшення прохань або припущень (напр., "We might go..."). |
| **Generally speaking** | Використовується для узагальнення, щоб уникнути занадто різких тверджень. |
| **Quite / A bit** | "Softeners", що зменшують інтенсивність прикметників (напр., "quite lucky" або "a bit reserved").|
