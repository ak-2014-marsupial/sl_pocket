# 46. Tap, swipe and pay

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cash** | Money in the form of coins or notes (paper money). | Готівка |
| **Change** | The money that you get back when you have paid for something with more money than it costs. | Решта |
| **Withdraw** | To take money out of a bank account or an ATM. | Знімати (гроші) |
| **ATM** | A machine that dispenses cash or performs other banking services when an account holder inserts a bank card. | Банкомат |
| **Wallet** | A small, flat case, often made of leather, for keeping money and credit cards in. | Гаманець |
| **Payment terminal** | A device used to process card payments at retail locations. | Платіжний термінал |
| **Tap** | To touch a credit card or phone on a terminal to make a contactless payment. | Прикласти (картку/телефон) |
| **Swipe** | To slide a credit card through a machine that reads the magnetic strip. | Провести (карткою) |
| **Bills** | Paper money (American English) or a statement of money owed for goods or services. | Купюри / Рахунки |
| **Debt** | An amount of money that you owe to a person, bank, or company. | Борг |
| **For free** | Without cost; costing nothing. | Безкоштовно |

## 2. Vocabulary Hooks

> To remember the difference: You **tap** like a gentle "knock" on the door, but you **swipe** like you are "sliding" on ice.
> Think of **ATM** as "Automatic Teller Machine" — it tells you how much money you can have!

## 3. Grammar Essentials

### Present Simple vs Present Continuous in Retail
Formula: `Subject + V1 (s/es)` (Simple) / `Subject + am/is/are + V-ing` (Continuous)
Вживаємо Present Simple для регулярних дій або правил оплати, а Present Continuous — для процесу, що відбувається прямо зараз біля каси.
*Example: I usually pay by card, but today I am paying with cash.*

### Modal Verbs for Requests (Polite English)
Formula: `Can/Could/May I + Verb...?`
Використовуємо для ввічливого запиту про спосіб оплати або допомогу.
*Example: Could I have the receipt, please?*

## 4. Written Practice

### True or False Task:
1. Cash is money in coins or paper. **True**
2. Bills are the extra money you get after paying. **False** (That is change)
3. You can withdraw money from an ATM. **True**
4. To tap a credit card means to touch it on a terminal to pay. **True**
5. A wallet is a machine for taking money out of your account. **False** (That is an ATM)
6. Debts mean you owe money to someone. **True**
7. A payment terminal is used to pay with a card. **True**
8. To swipe a credit card means to give money to a waiter. **False** (It means to slide the card through the machine)
9. If something is for free, you don’t need to pay for it. **True**
10. Change is the money you need to pay for services. **False** (Change is the balance returned to you)

### Translation Block:
- Я б хотів зняти трохи готівки в банкоматі. -> **I would like to withdraw some cash from the ATM.**
- Ви можете прикласти картку до терміналу тут. -> **You can tap your card on the terminal here.**
- Вибачте, я можу заплатити готівкою? -> **Excuse me, could I pay in cash?**

## 5. Speaking & Discussion Guide

**Q: Do you prefer to pay with cash or a credit card? Why?**
* **Positive:** "Actually, I prefer using a credit card because it’s much more convenient. I find that tapping my card is such a quick way to finish the transaction."
* **Neutral:** "To be honest, it depends on the situation. I might use cash for small purchases, but I usually prefer my card for larger bills."
* **Negative:** "I'm afraid I don't like using cards very much. I feel that using cash helps me track my spending more effectively."

**Q: Have you ever had a problem with a payment terminal? What happened?**
* **Positive:** "Fortunately, I haven't had many issues. Once, the terminal didn't recognize my card, but I just tried to swipe it instead of tapping, and it worked perfectly."
* **Neutral:** "Actually, I did have a small problem once. The connection was a bit slow, so it took a few minutes to process the payment."
* **Negative:** "I'm afraid I had a very awkward situation. The terminal was broken, and I didn't have enough cash, so I had to find an ATM nearby very quickly."

**Q: What’s the best thing you’ve ever received for free?**
* **Positive:** "Actually, the best thing was a professional course from my company. It was such a valuable experience and really helped my career."
* **Neutral:** "Perhaps it was a coffee from my favorite café. They have a loyalty program, so every tenth cup is for free."
* **Negative:** "To be honest, I can't think of anything significant. It might have been some promotional merchandise, but nothing very useful."

## 6. Professional Scenarios

**Scenario 1: At a Restaurant**
*Context: A customer is ready to pay the bill.*
**Role A (Server):** "Would you like to pay by card or in cash today?"
**Role B (Customer):** "I’d prefer to use my card, if possible. Could you bring the payment terminal to the table?"
**Role A (Server):** "Certainly. Please **tap** your card here... Great, would you like your receipt?"

**Scenario 2: At the Bank**
*Context: A client is having trouble with their card.*
**Role A (Bank Clerk):** "How can I help you today?"
**Role B (Client):** "Actually, I tried to **withdraw** money from the **ATM**, but my card was declined."
**Role A (Bank Clerk):** "I’m afraid there might be a temporary issue with your account. Let me check that for you."

## 7. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | When to use it (Context) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Could you please...?** | To ask for something (like a receipt or change) politely. |
| **I’d prefer to...** | To state your preference (e.g., card vs. cash) without being too direct. |
| **If possible** | To make a request sound less demanding. |
| **I'm afraid (that)...** | To explain a problem (e.g., a declined card or broken terminal). |
| **To be honest** | To share a personal preference or a difficult truth. |
| **Actually** | To introduce a new piece of information or correct a misunderstanding. |
