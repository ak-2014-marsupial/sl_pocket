# 44. Financial Fitness

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **To earn** |  To receive money as payment for work that you do. | Заробляти |
| **To spend** |  To give money as payment for something. | Витрачати |
| **To save** |  To keep money for use in the future rather than spending it now. | Заощаджувати |
| **To waste money** |  To spend money on things that are not necessary or useful. | Витрачати гроші на вітер |
| **To make a budget** |  To create a plan to show how much money a person or organization will earn and how it will be spent. | Складати бюджет |
| **To borrow** |  To get money from someone with the intention of giving it back after a period of time. | Брати в борг |
| **To lend** |  To give someone money for a period of time, expecting that they will then give it back. | Давати в борг |
| **To donate to a charity** |  To give money or goods to an organization that helps people in need. | Жертвувати на благодійність |
| **To do volunteer work** |  To do work that you are not paid for, usually to help people. | Займатись волонтерством |
| **Successful** |  Achieving the results that you wanted or hoped for. | Успішний |
| **Rich** |  Having a lot of money or valuable possessions. | Багатий |
| **Poor** |  Having very little money or very few possessions. | Бідний |

## 2. Vocabulary Hooks

>  "To **earn** a living, you must learn to **save** for a rainy day".
>  "Don't **waste** your potential by **spending** more than you **earn**".
>  "A **successful** person is one who can **lend** a hand without needing to **borrow**".

## 3. Grammar Essentials

**Present Simple for Financial Habits**
 Formula: `Subject + Verb (s/es for he/she/it) + Object`.
 Вживання: Використовується для опису регулярних фінансових звичок, постійних станів або загальновідомих фактів про гроші.

*  *Example: She earns a high salary working as an accountant.*
*  *Example: They save their income every month for emergencies.*
*  *Example: He wastes money on expensive gadgets.*

## 4. Written Practice

### Multiple Choice Exercises
1.  I **earn** money by working part-time at a coffee shop.
2.  She **spends** too much money on clothes she doesn’t wear.
3.  They **save** a portion of their income each month for their child’s education.
4.  He often **wastes** money on things he doesn’t really need.
5.  Every month, we sit down together to **make** a budget for our household.
6.  Can I **borrow** your book for the weekend?.
7.  I can **lend** you some money if you need it for your trip.
8.  They regularly **donate** to a charity that supports children’s education.
9.  She enjoys **doing** volunteer work at the local community center.
10.  He is a **successful** businessman with a successful business.
11.  The **poor** family received help from the local food bank.

### Translation Block
1. Вона витрачає багато грошей на книги та подорожі.  — **She spends a lot of money on books and travel.**
2. Ми складаємо бюджет на початку кожного місяця.  — **We make a budget at the beginning of each month.**
3. Можеш позичити гроші своєму кузену?  — **Can you lend money to your cousin?.**

## 5. Speaking & Discussion Guide

 **Q1: How do you avoid wasting money on unnecessary things? **
* **Positive:** To be honest, I find that making a strict shopping list could help me stay focused on what I actually need.
* **Neutral:** I try to think for a few days before buying something expensive, which might be a good way to avoid impulse purchases.
* **Negative:** I’m afraid I often struggle with this; actually, I sometimes buy things just because they are on sale.

**Q2: What does being successful mean to you?  Is it about money or something else? **
* **Positive:** Actually, I believe success is more about personal growth; however, having enough money to be independent would certainly be part of it.
* **Neutral:** It might be a combination of both; to be honest, a stable income is important, but so is having time for your family.
* **Negative:** I’m afraid I don’t think money makes you successful at all; I’d say it’s more about how much you could help others.

**Q3: You see something you really want, but it’s too expensive.  What do you do? **
*  **Positive:** I would definitely prefer to save money for a few weeks and then buy it.
*  **Neutral:** I might consider buying it with a credit card if I knew I could pay it back very soon.
*  **Negative:** Honestly, I would probably just borrow the money from a friend and pay it back little by little.

## 6. Professional Scenarios

 **Scenario: Planning a Budget **
Context: Alex and Maria are sitting down to manage their shared expenses for the upcoming month.
* **Role A (Alex):** Could we sit down and make a budget for next month? I’m afraid we spent a bit too much on dining out lately.
* **Role B (Maria):** I agree. Actually, it might be better if we allocate a specific amount for entertainment so we can save more for our trip.

 **Scenario: Borrowing and Lending **
Context: A friend is asking to borrow money for a specific purchase.
* **Role A (Maria):** To be honest, I found a great deal on a camera. Could I possibly borrow some money from you?
* **Role B (Alex):** I could lend you the money, but would you mind telling me when you might be able to pay it back?

## 7. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | When to use it (Context) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Actually / To be honest** |  Used to introduce a personal opinion or a fact that might be surprising. |
| **I’m afraid...** |  Used to politely introduce bad news or a disagreement. |
| **Could you... / Would you mind...** |  Used to make polite requests or ask for favors. |
| **It might be / It could be** |  Used to make a statement or suggestion sound less direct and more polite. |
| **I’d say... / In my view...** |  Used to express an opinion softly rather than as an absolute fact. |
