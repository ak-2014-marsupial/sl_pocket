#  Health Problems & Medical Advice

### 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **To feel sick** | To experience physical or mental exhaustion or illness. | Почуватися хворим |
| **To have a cold** | A common viral infection that causes sneezing and a runny nose. | Мати застуду |
| **To have the flu** | A highly contagious viral infection (influenza). | Хворіти на грип |
| **To sneeze** | To expel air forcibly from the nose due to irritation. | Чхати |
| **To cough** | To expel air from the lungs with a sudden sharp sound. | Кашляти |
| **Headache** | A continuous pain in the head. | Головний біль |
| **Stomachache** | Pain in the abdomen or stomach area. | Біль у шлунку |
| **Earache** | Pain inside the ear. | Біль у вусі |
| **Toothache** | Pain in or around a tooth. | Зубний біль |
| **Sore throat** | A condition where the throat feels dry, painful, or irritated. | Біль у горлі |
| **Allergy** | A damaging immune response by the body to a substance. | Алергія |

### 2. Grammar Essentials: Describing Aches

Для опису болю в англійській мові часто використовується дві конструкції:

1. **Body Part + ache:**
* *Formula:* `Subject + have/has + (a) + [Part of Body]ache`
* *Examples:* I have a **headache**. She has a **stomachache**. 

2. **Sore + Body Part:**
* *Formula:* `Subject + have/has + a sore + [Part of Body]`
* 
*Example:* I have a **sore throat**. 

**Sick vs. Ill (The Difference):** 

* **Sick:** Більш характерно для американської англійської; вживається для легких або тимчасових нездужань; неформальний стиль.
* **Ill:** Більш характерно для британської англійської; вживається для серйозних або тривалих хвороб; офіційний стиль.

### 3. Written Practice (Completed Exercises)

* When you start to **feel sick**, it’s best to rest and drink plenty of water. 

* It’s important to get a flu vaccine to avoid getting **the flu** during the winter. 

* During allergy season, I often **sneeze** because of the flowers. 

* The syrup helped reduce his **cough** at night. 

* Drinking hot tea with honey can calm down **a sore throat**. 

* After looking at the computer screen for too long, I got **a headache**. 

* Eating something unhealthy gave her **a stomachache**. 

* He couldn’t hear because of **an earache**. 

* She couldn’t concentrate in class because of **a toothache**.

### 4. Speaking & Discussion Guide

**Q1: What healthy habits do you have?** 

* *Positive:* "Actually, I try to stay active by walking at least 10,000 steps a day, and I always make sure to wash my hands before meals."
* *Neutral:* "To be honest, I don't have many strict habits, but I do try to drink enough water throughout the day, which might be helpful."
* *Negative:* "I'm afraid I haven't been very disciplined lately, although I do attempt to get at least seven hours of sleep when possible."

**Q2: What are the best ways to avoid getting sick?** 

* *Advice:* "I would suggest that getting a flu vaccine is quite essential. Also, it might be a good idea to maintain a balanced diet and avoid crowded places during the flu season."

### 5. Professional Scenarios (Dialogues)

**Scenario: At the Dentist's Office (Based on Emily's case)**

* **Dentist:** Good morning! How can I help you today?
* **Patient (Emily):** Good morning, Doctor. **I'm afraid** I've developed a terrible **toothache**. It started yesterday after I had some sweets.
* **Dentist:** I see. **Could you** tell me if the pain is constant?
* **Patient:** **Actually**, it hurts more when I try to eat anything cold.
* **Dentist:** I understand. You should avoid eating sweets for a while. I’ll examine it now, and we might need to do a filling.

**Scenario: A Consultation about Allergy (Based on Rebecca's case)**

* **Doctor:** Hello! What seems to be the problem?
* **Patient (Rebecca):** Hello. **To be honest**, I’ve been **sneezing** constantly since I received a bouquet of flowers this morning.
* **Doctor:** It sounds like you might have an **allergy**. **I would recommend** taking some medicine and, if possible, keeping the flowers in another room.

### 6. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase | Purpose (When to use) |
| --- | --- |
| **Actually...** | Щоб ввічливо уточнити інформацію або виправити співрозмовника. |
| **To be honest...** | Коли ви ділитеся особистою думкою або визнаєте щось (наприклад, шкідливу звичку). |
| **I’m afraid...** | Для ввічливого повідомлення поганих новин або висловлення занепокоєння. |
| **I would suggest / recommend...** | Для надання поради менш категоричним тоном. |
| **Could you...? / Would you mind...?** | Для ввічливих прохань. |
| **It sounds like you might have...** | Для висловлення припущення про діагноз (не звучить як остаточний вирок). |
