# 21. Gerund Phrases

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Avoid** | To stay away from someone or something; to prevent something from happening. | Уникати |
| **Can't help** | Used to say that someone is unable to change their behavior or feelings. | Не можу не / не можу стриматись |
| **Can't stand** | To strongly dislike someone or something. | Терпіти не можу |
| **Don't mind** | To not be annoyed or worried by something; to be happy to do something. | Не проти |
| **Imagine** | To form a mental image or concept of something. | Уявляти |
| **Consider** | To think about something carefully, especially before making a decision. | Вважати, розглядати |
| **Enjoy** | To take delight or pleasure in an activity or occasion. | Насолоджуватись |
| **Suggest** | To put forward an idea or plan for someone else to think about. | Пропонувати |

## 2. Vocabulary Hooks

> **Avoid** the noise to keep your poise.
> I **can't help** but yelp when I need help.
> I **can't stand** walking on hot sand.
> I **don't mind** being kind to all mankind.

## 3. Grammar Essentials

**Formula:** `Verb + -ing`

Герундій поєднує в собі властивості іменника та дієслова. Він не вживається з артиклями та може виступати в реченні як підмет або додаток.

**Spelling Rules:**
1. Якщо дієслово закінчується на німу **-e**, вона випадає: *make → making*.
2. Якщо дієслово закінчується на **-ie**, воно змінюється на **-y**: *die → dying*.
3. Подвоєння приголосної відбувається в односкладових дієсловах з короткою голосною: *get → getting*.

**Usage Cases:**
1. **As a Subject (як підмет):**
   *Example: Reading books helps me relax.*
2. **As an Object (як додаток):**
   *Example: I enjoy reading mystery novels.*
3. **After Prepositions (після прийменників):**
   *Example: He is afraid of flying alone.*
4. **After specific verbs (після певних дієслів):** avoid, enjoy, consider, suggest, etc.

## 4. Written Practice

### Exercise 1: Open the brackets
1. **Listening** to music makes me feel relaxed.
2. **Finding** a parking spot is not easy in a big city.
3. **Exercising** every morning is good for your health.
4. **Learning** a new language can be challenging.
5. **Growing** a garden takes a lot of patience and effort.

### Exercise 2: Verb Patterns
1. I can’t stand **waking up** early.
2. She enjoys **reading** novels in her free time.
3. They avoid **driving** because it’s stressful.
4. He doesn’t mind **donating** money if it’s for a good cause.
5. We consider **going** hiking every Saturday.

### Exercise 3: Correct Mistakes
1. She enjoys ~~to travel~~ **traveling** to new countries.
2. They consider ~~to move~~ **moving** to another city next year.
3. We can’t stand ~~to wait~~ **waiting** in long lines.

### Translation Block
* Я ненавиджу чекати в чергах. — **I hate waiting in lines.**
* Мені подобається займатися спортом щоранку. — **I enjoy exercising every morning.**
* Я розглядаю можливість вивчення нової мови. — **I’m considering learning a new language.**
* Вона часто уникає говорити про свої проблеми. — **She often avoids talking about her problems.**
* Я не проти допомогти тобі з домашнім завданням. — **I don’t mind helping you with your homework.**

## 5. Speaking & Discussion Guide

**Q: Do you enjoy traveling to new countries?**
* **Positive:** To be honest, I absolutely love visiting new places because exploring different cultures is truly enriching.
* **Neutral:** Actually, I find traveling quite interesting, though the long flights can be rather exhausting sometimes.
* **Negative:** I’m afraid I’m not very fond of traveling as I prefer the comfort and routine of my own home.

**Q: Is learning a new language challenging for you?**
* **Positive:** I’d say it is quite challenging; however, I believe that practicing every day makes the process much smoother.
* **Neutral:** To be fair, it has its ups and downs, but I wouldn't call it impossible if you have the right materials.
* **Negative:** Actually, I find it quite difficult to stay motivated, especially when it comes to memorizing complex grammar rules.

**Q: Do you mind working under pressure?**
* **Positive:** I don't mind it at all; in fact, I feel that working under pressure often helps me stay focused and productive.
* **Neutral:** Well, I could manage it if necessary, though I’d certainly prefer a more relaxed environment.
* **Negative:** To be honest, I find it quite stressful, so I try to avoid situations that require me to rush.

## 6. Professional Scenarios

**Context:** Discussing professional development and new responsibilities at work.

**Scenario 1:**
* **Role A:** Could you please tell me if you are interested in **taking on** more responsibilities?
* **Role B:** To be honest, I am quite interested in **learning** new skills, but I would appreciate some guidance initially.

**Scenario 2:**
* **Role A:** I was wondering if you would consider **leading** the new project team next month.
* **Role B:** I’m afraid I’m quite busy at the moment, but I wouldn't mind **assisting** the team lead if that helps.

## 7. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | When to use it (Context) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **To be honest / To be fair** | To introduce a personal opinion or a slightly negative point politely. |
| **I’m afraid...** | To politely disagree or deliver bad news / say "no". |
| **Actually...** | To gently correct someone or add a different perspective. |
| **I was wondering if...** | To make a request or ask a question in a less direct, softer way. |
| **Could / Would / Might** | To sound more professional and less demanding when asking for things. |
