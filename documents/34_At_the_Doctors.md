
# At the Doctor’s

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

| Word / Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад (Ukrainian) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **To feel well** | To be in good health  | Почуватися добре |
| **To feel sick / ill** | To feel unwell or as if you might vomit (Note: *Sick* is common in AmE, *Ill* in BrE) | Почуватися хворим |
| **To feel tired** | To be in need of rest or sleep  | Почуватися втомленим |
| **To feel nervous** | To feel worried or anxious  | Хвилюватися / нервувати |
| **To have a pain in...** | To experience physical suffering in a specific body part | Відчувати біль у... |
| **Stomachache** | Pain in the stomach  | Біль у животі |
| **Allergy** | A medical condition that causes a reaction to certain substances | Алергія |
| **Treatment** | Medical care given to a patient  | Лікування |

## 2. Grammar Essentials

У цій лекції ми розглядаємо вживання дієслова **to feel** (стан) та конструкції **to have a pain in** для опису самопочуття.

* **To feel + Adjective:**

*I feel tired.* (Я почуваюся втомленим).

*She feels nervous.* (Вона хвилюється).


* **To have a pain in + Body part:**
 
*I have a pain in my back.* (У мене болить спина).



* **Modal Verb "Should" for advice:**
 
*I think I **should** stay home today.* (Я думаю, мені варто залишитися вдома сьогодні).

*What **should** I do?* (Що мені варто робити?).

## 3. Written Practice 

Exercise 1: Fill in the gaps 

1. I feel tired in the morning because I wake up at 5 AM.

2. It's cold and snowy outside. I feel **ill**! 

3. My little sister feels sick. She has a **stomachache**.


Exercise 2: Multiple Choice 

1. I feel **tired**. I want to sleep.

2. My stomach hurts. I feel **sick**.

3. I always feel **nervous** when I tell the doctor about my symptoms. I am afraid of hospitals.

4. I don’t feel well. What should I **do**? 

5. The doctor says, “Where does it **hurt**?” 


Exercise 3: Translation into English 

1. I have an allergy to flowers / I am allergic to flowers.

2. You have a pain in your back because you don’t sleep well.

3. I feel sick because of bad food.

4. She feels nervous because she has an exam today.

5. We don’t feel well. We are very tired.

6. He has a pain in his stomach. He can’t eat.

## 4. Speaking & Discussion Guide

Discussion Questions 

**Q1: How do you feel today?** 

* **Positive:** *Actually, I feel quite energetic today, although I’ve been very busy lately.*
* **Neutral:** *To be honest, I feel okay, but I might be a little bit tired after work.*
* **Negative:** *I'm afraid I don't feel very well today. I think I might be coming down with something.*

**Q2: What do you do to stay healthy?** 

* **Positive:** *I try to exercise regularly and, if possible, I prefer eating home-cooked meals.*
* **Neutral:** *I suppose I try to drink enough water, but I could definitely sleep more.*
* **Negative:** *To be fair, I haven't been very active recently, though I know I should change that.*

**Q3: What should you do if you don’t feel well?** 

* **Advice 1:** *I believe it would be best to stay at home and rest for a day or two.*
* **Advice 2:** *If the pain persists, you might want to consider making an appointment with a specialist.*

### Professional Dialogue Scenarios

**Scenario: At the Clinic Reception**

* **Patient:** *Good morning. I was wondering if it would be possible to see a doctor today? I don't feel very well.*
* **Receptionist:** *I'm sorry to hear that. Could you please tell me where it hurts?*
* **Patient:** *Actually, I have a persistent pain in my stomach and I feel quite sick.*

## 5. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | How to use it |
| --- | --- |
| **Actually / To be honest** | Used to introduce a personal opinion or a correction politely. |
| **I’m afraid...** | Used to introduce bad news or a negative state (e.g., "I'm afraid I feel ill"). |
| **I was wondering if...** | A very polite way to ask for something or make a request. |
| **You might want to...** | A "soft" way to give advice instead of saying "You must". |
| **Could / Would / Might** | Modal verbs that make questions and statements sound less demanding. |
| **I suppose / I believe** | Used to express an opinion without sounding too aggressive or certain. |
