# Past Simple Passive

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions
| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Active voice** |  A grammatical voice where the subject performs the action. | Активний стан |
| **Passive voice** |  A grammatical voice where the action's target is the focus of the sentence. | Пасивний стан |
| **Postpone** |  To arrange for something to take place at a time later than that first scheduled. | Відкладати, переносити |
| **Deliver** |  To take letters, parcels, etc. to a person or an office. | Доставляти |
| **Repair** |  To fix something that is broken, damaged, or not working. | Ремонтувати |
| **Direct** |  To control or be in charge of the making of a film. | Режисирувати |
| **Sweep** |  To clean something, especially a floor, by using a brush. | Підмітати |
| **Fix** |  To repair something that is broken or not working properly. | Чинити, ремонтувати |

## 2. Vocabulary Hooks
>  I’m afraid I have to **postpone** our meeting; let’s put it off until we both have more time.
>  To be honest, the package wasn't **delivered** on time, but it should arrive by tomorrow morning.

## 3. Grammar Essentials
 **Formula:** `Subject + was/were + Verb3 (Past Participle)` 

 Past Simple Passive використовується, коли ми хочемо змістити фокус із того, хто виконав дію, на саму дію або об'єкт, над яким вона була вчинена. 
* **Was** використовується з одниною (I, he, she, it).
*  **Were** використовується з множиною (we, you, they).

 *Example: The bridge was built in 2005.*
 *Example: The letters were delivered this morning.*
 *Example: The car wasn't fixed yesterday.*

## 4. Written Practice
1.  They built the bridge in 2005. → **The bridge was built in 2005**.
2. She wrote the report last night.  → **The report was written last night**.
3. The gardener planted the flowers.  → **The flowers were planted by the gardener**.
4. They closed the store at 8 PM.  → **The store was closed at 8 PM**.
5. The team won the match.  → **The match was won by the team**.
6. The chef cooked the meal.  → **The meal was cooked by the chef**.
7. He repaired the computer.  → **The computer was repaired by him**.
8. They decorated the hall for the event.  → **The hall was decorated for the event**.
9. She cleaned the windows.  → **The windows were cleaned by her**.

**Translation Block**
1. Лист був надісланий.  — **The letter was sent**.
2. Торт був спечений.  — **The cake was baked**.
3. Фільм був переглянутий.  — **The movie was watched**.
4. Кімната була прибрана.  — **The room was cleaned**.

## 5. Speaking & Discussion Guide
 **Q: What kind of present was given to you recently? **
* **Positive:** Actually, I was given a wonderful leather-bound journal for my birthday, which I found quite thoughtful.
* **Neutral:** To be honest, a simple gift card was given to me, which is practical, though perhaps not very personal.
* **Negative:** I’m afraid a present was given to me that I already owned, but I certainly appreciated the gesture nonetheless.

 **Q: What thing was fixed in your home lately?**
* **Positive:** Could I say that the kitchen faucet was finally fixed last weekend? It has made a huge difference.
* **Neutral:** Well, the old wooden chair in the hallway was repaired by a local craftsman recently.
* **Negative:** Actually, the air conditioner was supposed to be fixed, but I'm afraid the technician couldn't find the necessary parts.

 **Q: What movie was shown at your local cinema recently? **
* **Positive:** I believe a very interesting historical documentary was shown, and it was quite well-received by the audience.
* **Neutral:** A new superhero movie was shown last week, though it didn't really catch my attention, to be honest.
* **Negative:** Unfortunately, a rather poorly reviewed action film was shown, which I might skip if I were you.

## 6. Professional Scenarios
**Context: Reporting on a completed project task in the office.**
**Role A:** Could you please tell me if the monthly report was finished on time?
 **Role B:** To be honest, it was completed last night and was already sent to the manager this morning.

**Context: Discussing a delay in a business meeting.**
**Role A:** I’m afraid the meeting room isn't ready yet. Has it been cleaned?
 **Role B:** Actually, the room was cleaned an hour ago, but the meeting was postponed until next week due to some technical issues.

## 7. Polite English Cheat Sheet
| Phrase / Softener | When to use it (Context) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Actually** | Use this to gently correct someone or add a new piece of information. |
| **To be honest** | Use this when you are sharing a personal opinion or a slightly negative fact. |
| **I’m afraid** | Use this to introduce bad news or a disappointment in a soft way. |
| **Could / Would / Might** | Use these modal verbs to make requests or suggestions sound more polite. |
| **Could I say that...** | Use this as an indirect way to start sharing your thoughts. |
