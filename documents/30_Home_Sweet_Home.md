# Home, Sweet Home

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions


| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Furniture** | Objects such as tables, chairs, or beds that are used in a room. | Меблі |
| **Wardrobe** | A tall cupboard or piece of furniture in which you can hang your clothes. | Шафа (для одягу) |
| **Cupboard** | A piece of furniture with shelves and doors, used for storing plates, cups, or food. | Шафка, сервант |
| **Bedside table** | A small table that is kept next to a bed. | Столик біля ліжка |
| **Dishwasher** | A machine that washes dishes automatically. | Посудомийна машина |
| **Washing machine** | A machine used for washing clothes. | Пральна машина |
| **Microwave** | An electric oven that uses waves to heat food very quickly. | Мікрохвильова піч |
| **Fridge** | A large container that is kept cool inside, used for preserving food. | Холодильник |
| **Stove / Cooker** | A large piece of equipment for cooking food. | Кухонна плита |
| **Sofa** | A long comfortable seat with a back and arms for two or more people. | Диван |
| **Carpet** | A thick material used for covering floors. | Килим |
| **Mirror** | A piece of glass that reflects images. | Дзеркало |
| **Balcony** | An area with a wall or bars around it, joined to the outside wall of a building. | Балкон |

## 2. Grammar Essentials: Describing a Home

**There is + singular noun:** "There is a washing machine in the kitchen".

**There are + plural noun:** "There are a lot of plates and plants".

* **Questions:** "Is there a fridge?" — "Yes, there is / No, there isn't".

3. We have a comfortable **sofa** in our living room.

### Exercise: True or False

* The mirror is on the floor. — **False** (It is usually on the wall).

* There is a bed in the bedroom. — **True**.

* There is a fridge in the bathroom. — **False** (It belongs in the kitchen).

* There are books in the fridge. — **False**.

## 4. Speaking & Discussion Guide

**Question 1: Do you share housework with friends or family?** 

* *Positive:* "Actually, I do share the chores with my family. We usually try to split them fairly so that no one feels overwhelmed."
* *Neutral:* "To be honest, we don't have a strict schedule, but we might help each other out when someone is particularly busy."
* *Negative:* "I'm afraid I live alone at the moment, so I handle all the housework myself, though I wouldn't mind some help!"

**Question 2: What household chore do you dislike the most and why?** 

* *Answer:* "I’d have to say that I particularly dislike doing the dishes. It feels quite never-ending, though using a dishwasher could certainly make it more bearable."

Question 3: Would you like to live in a house or in an apartment? Why? 

* *Answer:* "That’s a great question. I think I would prefer living in a house because it offers more privacy. However, an apartment might be more convenient for someone working in the city center."

## 5. Professional Dialogue Scenarios

**Scenario: Deciding on House Chores**

* **Person A:** "I was wondering if we could perhaps discuss how we share the housework? It seems things are getting a bit messy."
* **Person B:** "Actually, you're right. To be honest, I've been quite busy lately. Would you mind if I do the laundry, and you take out the trash?"
* **Person A:** "That sounds fair. Could we also agree to clean up the living room together on Saturdays?"

## 6. Polite English Cheat Sheet

Використовуйте ці фрази, щоб звучати ввічливіше (як у прикладах вище):

| Phrase / Softener | When to use it |
| --- | --- |
| **Actually / To be honest** | Використовуйте для пом'якшення прямої заяви або незгоди. |
| **I'm afraid...** | Для ввічливого повідомлення поганих новин або відмови. |
| **Could you / Would you mind...** | Для створення ввічливого прохання замість наказу. |
| **I’d have to say...** | Додає невпевненості та ввічливості вашій думці. |
| **Perhaps / Might** | Допомагає зробити ваші пропозиції менш категоричними. |
