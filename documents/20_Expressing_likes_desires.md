# 20. Expressing likes, desires and preferences

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **In general** |  Usually; in most situations.  | Загалом |
| **Habit** |  A settled or regular tendency or practice. | Звичка |
| **Specific situation** |  A particular set of circumstances.  | Конкретна ситуація |
| **Desire** |  A strong feeling of wanting to have something or wishing for something to happen.  | Бажання |
| **Polite request** |  A way of asking for something in a respectful and civil manner.  | Ввічливе прохання |
| **Stronger preference** |  A choice that is more favored than other options.  | Більша перевага |
| **Direct manner** |  Speaking or acting in a straightforward way.  | Пряма манера |
| **Weekend getaway** |  A short trip taken on a Saturday and Sunday.  | Поїздка на вихідні |

## 2. Vocabulary Hooks

>  I **like swimming** because the water feels like a **general** hug from nature. 
>  I **would like to order** a coffee to satisfy my morning **desire** for caffeine. 
>  I **would rather watch** a movie than talk; it is my **direct** way of relaxing. 

## 3. Grammar Essentials

**1. General Preferences**
 Formula: `Subject + like + Verb-ing` 
 Вживається для опису того, що нам подобається або чому ми віддаємо перевагу в цілому. 
 *Example: I like swimming.* 

**2. Habits and Specific Situations**
 Formula: `Subject + like + to + Verb` 
 Вживається для опису звичок або вподобань у конкретних ситуаціях. 
 *Example: I like to read before going to bed.* 

**3. Desires and Polite Requests**
 Formula: `Subject + would like + to + Verb` 
 Вживається для вираження бажання або ввічливого прохання. 
 *Example: I would like to order a coffee.* 

**4. Strong Preferences**
 Formula: `Subject + would prefer + to + Verb` [cite: 12]
 Вживається для вираження сильної переваги одного варіанту над іншим. [cite: 12]
 *Example: I would prefer to stay at home tonight.* [cite: 12]

**5. Direct Preferences**
 Formula: `Subject + would rather + Verb` 
 Вживається для прямого вираження вподобань (після "rather" частка "to" не ставиться). 
 *Example: I would rather watch a movie than read a book.* 

## 4. Written Practice

 **Exercise 1: Open the brackets** 
1.  I **like reading** books about history in my free time. 
2.  She **would like to try** a new restaurant this weekend. 
3.  They **like watching** movies together on Friday nights. 
4.  We **would prefer to stay** at home rather than go out tonight. 
5.  He **would rather play** video games than study. 
6.  I **like cooking** dinner for my family every evening. 
7.  She **would rather not go** to the beach when it’s crowded. 
8.  They **like travelling** abroad during their holidays. 
9.  He **would like to learn** how to drive a car. 
10.  I **would prefer not to eat** spicy food. 

 **Exercise 2: Correct the mistakes** 
1.  I like **swimming** in the pool every weekend. 
2.  She would prefer **to go** to the gym in the morning. 
3.  I would like **to go** to a concert next weekend. 
4.  They like **playing** tennis and running in the park. 
5.  I would rather eat pizza than **cook** pasta. 

### Translation Block

* Я люблю кататися на велосипеді в парку.  -> **I like cycling in the park.** 
* Він би хотів поїхати до Парижа наступного літа.  -> **He would like to go to Paris next summer.** 
* Ми віддаємо перевагу їсти вдома, а не в ресторані.  -> **We would prefer to eat at home rather than in a restaurant.** 
* Я люблю читати книги вночі.  -> **I like reading books at night.** 
* Вона б радше гуляла в парку, ніж сиділа вдома.  -> **She would rather walk in the park than stay at home.** 

## 5. Speaking & Discussion Guide

 **Q: Where would you prefer to go on vacation: to a historical city or a tropical island?** 
*  **Positive:** To be honest, I would prefer to visit a tropical island like Bali because I actually enjoy warm weather and relaxing by the sea. 
*  **Neutral:** Actually, I could go either way, but I might prefer a historical city this time to see some famous landmarks. 
* **Negative:** I’m afraid I wouldn’t like to go to a tropical island as I find the heat a bit too much;  I’d rather stay in a cooler city. 

 **Q: What would you like to do in your free time: take up painting or learn the guitar?** 
*  **Positive:** I would like to learn how to play the guitar because I really enjoy music and think it would be fun. 
* **Neutral:** I might like to try painting, although I’m not very artistic.  It could be a nice way to relax. 
* **Negative:** To be honest, I would rather not start either of those;  I’d prefer to spend my free time reading. 

 **Q: What would you rather do tonight: eat out at a restaurant or order fast food?**
*  **Positive:** I would rather eat fast food at home because it’s quick and I’d like to have a quiet evening. 
*  **Neutral:** I could go to a restaurant if you like, but ordering in sounds just as good to me. 
*  **Negative:** I’m afraid I don’t really like fast food, so I would prefer to cook something healthy at home instead. 

## 6. Professional Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Choosing a Meeting Venue**
 *Context: Two colleagues are deciding where to hold their weekly catch-up.* 
 **Role A:** Would you prefer to have our meeting in the library or at the café down the street? 
 **Role B:** To be honest, I would prefer to study and work in the library because it’s quiet and I can concentrate better. 

**Scenario 2: Planning a Team Activity**
 *Context: Discussing weekend plans with a colleague.* 
 **Role A:** What do you like doing on weekends to relax after work? 
 **Role B:** I actually like going for a run in the park because it helps me clear my head. 

**Scenario 3: Corporate Gift Choice**
 *Context: A manager asking an employee about a reward preference.* 
 **Role A:** For your anniversary, would you like to receive a new watch or a shopping voucher? 
 **Role B:** I would like to receive the voucher, if you don't mind, because I enjoy choosing my own items. 

## 7. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | When to use it (Context) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Actually** |  To introduce a personal preference or gently correct an assumption. 
| **To be honest** |  To express a sincere opinion or a preference that might differ from others.  |
| **I’m afraid...** |  To politely decline an invitation or express a negative preference. |
| **Would like to...** |  To make a polite request or express a desire in a professional way.  |
| **Would prefer to...** |  To state a choice between two options more formally.  |
| **Could / Might** |  To express a neutral or uncertain preference politely.  |
