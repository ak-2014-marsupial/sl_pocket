### **Grammar Essentials**

* **Describing appearance (Have/Has):**
* *Formula:* Subject + have/has + Adjectives + Feature.
 
*Example:* She **has** blonde hair and blue eyes.
 
*Example:* Sarah’s sister **is** ginger and **has** freckles.

* **Asking questions:**
* *Formula:* Does + Subject + have + Feature?
 
*Example:* **Does** he **have** a beard? 


* **Describing body shape (To be):**
 
*Example:* Oleksandr **is** in good shape.
 
*Example:* The baby **is** chubby.

### **Speaking & Discussion Guide**

#### **Questions and Answers (Polite Style)**

Q1: What features do you find most attractive in a person? 

* **Positive:** *Actually, I believe that a kind smile and expressive eyes are the most attractive features. To be honest, I’ve always found curly hair quite charming as well.*
* **Neutral:** *I suppose it depends on the person, but I might say that a straight nose and a well-defined face shape are generally considered quite attractive.*
* **Negative:** *I’m afraid I don’t really focus on specific physical features. To be honest, I think personality matters much more than whether someone has freckles or a beard.*

**Q2: What kind of weather do you prefer? (Extended answer)**

* **Positive:** *I’d say I’m quite fond of sunny and warm weather. It might sound cliché, but I find it much easier to stay active and be in good shape when the sun is out.*
* **Negative:** *To be honest, I’m not a big fan of the heat. I’m afraid I find it quite uncomfortable, so I actually prefer cool, rainy days when I can stay indoors.*

#### **Practice Scenarios (Professional Dialogues)**

**Scenario 1: Describing a colleague for a meeting**

* **A:** Could you tell me who I should look for at the conference?
* **B:** Of course. To be honest, she is quite easy to spot. She has long brunette hair and usually wears it straight. She is in good shape and has a very friendly, round face.

**Scenario 2: Identifying a person from a photo**

* **A:** Is this the person you were talking about?
* **B:** I’m afraid not. Actually, the gentleman I mentioned has a beard and a slightly humped nose. This person, however, has a very straight nose and no facial hair at all.

### **Polite English Cheat Sheet**

| Phrase/Softener | When to use it |
| --- | --- |
| **Actually / To be honest** | Use these to introduce a different opinion or to correct someone gently without being rude. |
| **I’m afraid...** | Use this to deliver negative news or to disagree politely (e.g., "I'm afraid I don't agree"). |
| **Could you / Would you** | Use these modal verbs to make requests sound less demanding and more professional. |
| **I might say / I’d say** | Use these to express an opinion less strongly, making it sound more like a suggestion than an absolute fact. |
| **Quite / Rather** | Use these to soften adjectives (e.g., "It's quite difficult" sounds better than "It's difficult"). |
