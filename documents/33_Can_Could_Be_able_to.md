# Can, Could, Be able to 
---
### **Vocabulary & Definitions**

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад українською |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Ability** | The physical or mental power to do something. | Здатність / вміння |
| **To count** | To say numbers in order or calculate the total. | Рахувати |
| **To drive** | To operate and control the direction and speed of a motor vehicle. | Водити (авто) |
| **To explain** | To make something clear or easy to understand by describing it in more detail. | Пояснювати |
| **To attend** | To be present at an event, meeting, or function.  | Відвідувати / бути присутнім |
| **To pass a test** | To be successful in an exam or competitive situation. | Скласти іспит |
| **Instruction** | Detailed information telling how something should be done. | Інструкція |

### **Grammar Essentials**

#### **1. Can (Present Ability)**

Використовується для опису вмінь у теперішньому часі. 

**Formula:** `Subject + can + V1` 

**Example:** *I can swim every day.* 

#### **2. Could (Past Ability)**

Використовується для опису вмінь у минулому. 

**Formula:** `Subject + could + V1` 

**Example:** *When I was 6, I could run very fast.* 


#### **3. Be able to (All Tenses)**

Універсальна конструкція, яка особливо корисна для майбутнього часу або специфічних випадків у минулому. 

**Present:** `am/is/are + able to + V1` 

**Past:** `was/were + able to + V1` 

**Future:** `will be able to + V1` 

**Example:** *I will be able to drive next year.*

### **Written Practice**

**Exercise: Fill the gaps with 'can' or 'could'**

1. I **can swim** every day. 

2. She **can’t drive** a car. 

3. **Can** they **play** the guitar? 

4. We **can help** you with your homework. 

5. He **can’t study** tonight because he’s tired. 

6. **Can** you **explain** this for me, please? 

7. My sister **can speak** three languages. 

8. When I was 6, I **could run** very fast. 

9. She **could speak** French when she was a child. 

10. We **couldn’t find** the answer, so the teacher helped us. 

11. **Could** you **hear** the music from outside last night? 

12. He **couldn’t pass** the test because he didn’t study. 

13. My grandmother **could play** the piano beautifully. 


**Exercise: Be able to in different tenses**

1. I **am able to speak** English very well. 

2. She **was able to finish** her homework yesterday. 

3. They **will be able to come** tomorrow. 

4. He **wasn’t able to drive** last week. 

5. **Are** you **able to help** me now? 

6. We **will not be able to attend** the meeting next Monday. 

7. **Was** she **able to understand** the instructions yesterday? 


**Block: Translation**

* Я можу допомогти тобі зараз. — *I can help you now.* 

* Вона могла добре плавати в дитинстві. — *She could swim well when she was a child.* 

* Ми зможемо зустрітись завтра. — *We will be able to meet tomorrow.* 

* Ти зможеш пояснити мені це завдання? — *Can you explain this task to me?* 

* Вона змогла знайти свою сумку вчора. — *She was able to find her bag yesterday.*

### **Speaking & Discussion Guide**

1. Can you ride a bike? 

* **Positive:** *Actually, I can ride a bike quite well. I’ve enjoyed cycling since I was a child, and I find it to be a very relaxing hobby.*
* **Negative:** *To be honest, I’m afraid I can’t. I never really had the chance to learn, though I might consider taking some lessons in the future.*
* **Neutral:** *I can ride a bike, but I wouldn’t say I’m an expert. I usually only cycle occasionally when the weather is particularly nice.*

2. Could you count quickly when you were a child? 

* **Positive:** *Yes, I could count very quickly. As far as I remember, math was one of my favorite subjects, so I was always quite good with numbers.*
* **Negative:** *I'm afraid I couldn't. To be fair, I found arithmetic a bit challenging when I was younger, so I usually needed more time.*
* **Neutral:** *I suppose I could count at a normal pace, but I might have struggled with more complex calculations back then.*

3. Will you be able to speak English fluently in the future? 

* **Positive:** *I certainly believe so. If I continue practicing every day, I’m confident I’ll be able to communicate fluently and professionally.*
* **Negative:** *It’s hard to say, really. I’m quite busy at the moment, so I’m afraid it might take a bit longer than I originally planned.*
* **Neutral:** *I hope to be able to speak it well enough for my career. It might be a challenge, but I’m willing to put in the effort.*

### **Polite English Cheat Sheet**

| Phrase/Softener | How to use it |
| --- | --- |
| **Actually...** | Використовуйте, щоб ввічливо виправити когось або додати неочікувану інформацію. |
| **To be honest...** | Допомагає пом'якшити негативну відповідь або висловити щиру думку. |
| **I’m afraid...** | Використовується для ввічливої відмови або повідомлення поганих новин. |
| **Could/Would/Might** | Робить прохання або припущення менш прямими та більш ввічливими. |
| **I suppose...** | Допомагає висловити невпевненість або обережне припущення. |
| **Could you...?** | Більш ввічлива форма наказового способу. Замість "Explain this", скажіть "Could you explain this for me, please?".
