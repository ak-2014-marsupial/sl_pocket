# 22. Life Journey: Important Milestones

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **To grow up** |  To become older and more mature  | Вирости, ставати дорослим |
| **To graduate from university** |  To finish university and receive a diploma  | Закінчити університет |
| **To get a job** |  To find work and start working  | Влаштуватися на роботу |
| **To move** |  To change the place where you live  | Переїхати |
| **To date someone** |  To have a romantic relationship with someone  | Зустрічатися з кимось |
| **To get engaged** |  To make a formal promise to get married | Заручитися |
| **To get married** |  To begin a legal relationship as husband and wife  | Одружитися / Вийти заміж |
| **To get divorced** |  To legally end a marriage  | Розлучитися |
| **To break up** |  To end a romantic relationship  | Розійтися / Розірвати стосунки |
| **To die** |  To stop living  | Померти |

## 2. Vocabulary Hooks

>  As a child, I wanted to become a professional when I **grow up**. 
>  After school, I hope to **get a job** and make money. 
>  We need to **move** to a new house next month. 

## 3. Grammar Essentials

**Past Simple for Life Events**
Formula: `Subject + Verb-ed / V2`
Використовується для опису завершених подій у минулому, таких як дати закінчення навчання, весілля чи зміна місця проживання.
 *Example: My parents **got married** when they were very young. *
 *Example: My aunt and uncle **got divorced** last year. *

**Future Intentions with "Want to" & "Hope to"**
Formula: `Subject + want/hope + to + Verb`
Використовується для вираження планів на майбутнє та мрій щодо кар'єри чи способу життя.
 *Example: I want to **grow up** and travel the world. *
 *Example: Most people want to **graduate** and start their careers. *

## 4. Written Practice

1.  Last year, my cousin **got a job** as a teacher. 
2.  After high school, most people want to **graduate from university** and start their careers. 
3.  When you **move** to a new city, you may need to find a new job. 
4.  It is quite reasonable **to date someone** for a period of time before marriage. 
5.  In childhood, each of us dreamed **of growing up** as quickly as possible. 

**Translation Block**
- A: Коли твої батьки **одружилися**?  -> A: When did your parents **get married**? 
-  B: Вони одружилися, коли їм було 25. -> B: They **got married** when they were 25. 
- A: Я чув, що ви з Люсі більше не разом.  -> A: I heard you and Lucy are not together anymore. 
- B: Так, ми вирішили **розійтися** минулого місяця.  -> B: Yes, we decided to **break up** last month. 

## 5. Speaking & Discussion Guide

 **Q: How can moving to another country change a person's life? **
* **Positive:** To be honest, I believe it’s a life-changing opportunity. You could definitely broaden your horizons and gain international career experience.
* **Neutral:** Actually, it might depend on the person. While some people find it exciting, others might struggle with the cultural adjustment at first.
* **Negative:** I’m afraid it can be quite stressful. Moving away from family and friends would likely lead to feeling quite lonely in a new environment.

 **Q: What did you want to become when you grow up? **
* **Positive:** As a child, I really wanted to become an engineer. I would always build things and I'm quite happy that I pursued this path.
* **Neutral:** Actually, I had many different dreams. At one point, I thought about becoming a pilot, but later I changed my mind.
* **Negative:** To be honest, I wasn't really sure back then. I might have followed my parents' advice rather than having a specific dream of my own.

## 6. Professional Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Sharing News about a Colleague**
*Context: Two colleagues are talking in the breakroom about a mutual friend.*
*  **Role A:** Did you see the ring on Emily’s finger? 
*  **Role B:** Yes, actually, she and Tom just **got engaged**! 
* **Role A:** Oh, that’s wonderful!  I’m so happy for them. 

**Scenario 2: Discussing Career Transitions**
*Context: An interview or a networking event where someone describes their background.*
* **Role A:** Could you tell me about your background after you finished your studies?
* **Role B:** Certainly.  Right after **graduation**, I started working at a local tech firm to gain some practical experience. 
* **Role A:** That sounds like a very solid start to your career.

## 7. Polite English Cheat Sheet

| Phrase / Softener | When to use it (Context) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Actually** | To introduce a fact or clarify a situation politely. |
| **To be honest** | To share a personal opinion or a slightly negative view. |
| **I’m afraid** | To deliver disappointing news or disagree gently. |
| **Could / Would / Might** | To make questions or statements sound less direct and more professional. |
| **That’s wonderful / I’m so happy** |  To react positively to someone's good news (e.g., marriage or engagement).  |
| **I’m sorry to hear that / That’s very sad** |  To express empathy when someone shares news of a breakup or death.  |
