
# "Get" Phrases


### 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

Дієслово **get** є одним із найуживаніших в англійській мові завдяки своїй багатофункціональності. Нижче наведено основні значення та сталі вирази.

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад (Ukrainian) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **To get (receive)** | To be given or sent something. | Отримувати |
| **To get (buy)** | To obtain something by paying money. | Купувати |
| **To get (reach)** | To arrive at a particular place. | Добиратися |
| **To get (become)** | To change into a new state or condition. | Ставати (переходити в стан) |
| **Get better** | To recover from an illness or improve. | Одужувати / Ставати краще |
| **Get bored** | To feel weary and restless through lack of interest. | Занудьгувати |
| **Get angry with** | To become annoyed or lose one's temper with someone. | Розсердитися на когось |
| **Get hungry** | To start feeling the need for food. | Зголодніти |
| **Get ill** | To become sick or unhealthy. | Захворіти |
| **Get worried** | To become anxious about a problem or situation. | Розхвилюватися |
| **Get thirsty** | To feel a need to drink. | Хотіти пити |
| **Get tired of** | To become exhausted or bored with something. | Втомитися від чогось |

---

### 2. Grammar Essentials

**The Logic of "Get" as "Become":**
Коли ми використовуємо *get* з прикметником, це означає процес зміни стану (transition).

* **Formula:** Subject + Get (in correct tense) + Adjective
* **Present Continuous (Ongoing change):** "It is **getting** cold." (Стає холодно — процес триває) .


* **Past Simple (Completed change):** "I **got** angry." (Я розсердився — стан змінився у минулому) .


* **Common Error Alert:** Не використовуйте "gooder". Правильно: **"My English is getting better"**.

### 3. Written Practice (Completed Exercises)


**Exercise 1: Fill in the blanks**

1. I hope you **get better** soon after your cold.


2. We **got bored** during the long meeting.


3. After walking for an hour, I **got hungry**.


4. She **got angry with** her brother because he broke her toy.


5. He **got tired of** waiting and left the café.


6. After running for an hour, I always **get thirsty**.


7. She started to **get worried** when he didn’t answer the phone.


8. I hope I don’t **get ill** before my vacation.



**Exercise 4: Error Correction (Professional Polish)**

1. She **got angry with** her friends because they were late.


2. They **get ill** every winter because of the cold weather.


3. I **got bored** at the party, so I left early.


4. After the long run, he **got thirsty** and drank a lot of water.


5. He always **gets hungry** before lunchtime.


6. I **got tired of** doing the same work every day.


7. My English **is getting better**.

### 4. Speaking & Discussion Guide


**Q1: What do you do to get better when you have a cold?** 

* **Positive/Proactive:** "Actually, I find that the best way to recover is to get plenty of rest. I might also drink a lot of herbal tea to stay hydrated."
* **Neutral:** "To be honest, I usually just take some vitamins and wait for it to pass. It could take a few days, but it generally works."

**Q2: What activities do you do when you get bored?** 

* **Active:** "If I’m honest, I don’t get bored often, but when I do, I might go for a walk. It actually helps me clear my head."
* **Passive:** "I’m afraid I tend to just scroll through my phone, though I would prefer to read a book if I had the energy."

**Q3: What usually makes you get angry?** 

* **Professional:** "I might get a bit frustrated when people are late for meetings without a valid reason. It’s actually quite important for me to stay on schedule."
* **Personal:** "To be honest, I get a little bit angry when I lose my keys. It’s a bit silly, but it can be quite annoying in the morning."

**Q4: What do you think is the best way to avoid getting ill?** 

* **Balanced:** "In my opinion, the most effective way might be maintaining a healthy diet. To be honest, regular exercise also plays a huge role in staying fit."

### 5. Dialogue Scenario: Professional Workplace

*Scenario: Two colleagues discussing a project delay.*

**A:** "I’m afraid I’m **getting** a bit **worried** about the deadline. We haven't **received** the data yet."

**B:** "Actually, I **got** an email from the team this morning. They are **getting tired of** the software bugs, but they promised to finish it soon."

**A:** "That’s good to hear. I was starting to **get** a little **angry with** the lack of communication."

**B:** "I understand. Would it be helpful if I checked in with them again later?"

### 6. Polite English Cheat Sheet

Використовуйте ці фрази, щоб звучати професійно та ввічливо.

| Phrase/Softener | When to use |
| --- | --- |
| **"Actually..."** | Щоб ввічливо виправити когось або додати нову інформацію. |
| **"To be honest..."** | Щоб висловити свою думку, яка може бути негативною або неочікуваною. |
| **"I’m afraid..."** | Щоб повідомити погані новини або висловити незгоду. |
| **"I might / It could..."** | Щоб зробити ваші твердження менш категоричними (дистанціювання). |
| **"Would it be possible / Could you..."** | Для створення ввічливих запитань замість прямих наказів. |
| **"A bit / A little bit"** | Використовуйте перед прикметниками (angry, worried), щоб пом'якшити негативні емоції. |
