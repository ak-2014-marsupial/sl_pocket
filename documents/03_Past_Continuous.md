# Past Continuous

## 1. Vocabulary & Definitions

У цьому розділі зібрано ключові слова та часові маркери, які допомагають ідентифікувати тривалу дію в минулому.

| Word/Phrase | Definition in English | Переклад |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **All day / The whole day** | Throughout the entire duration of the day. | Весь день |
| **While** | During the time that something else is happening. | В той час як / поки |
| **At 6 PM (yesterday)** | A specific point in time in the past. | О 6 годині вечора (вчора) |
| **At the same time** | Simultaneously; happening together. | В цей же час |
| **Sibling** | A person's brother or sister. | Брат або сестра |
| **Spouse** | A husband or wife, considered as a partner in a marriage. | Чоловік або дружина |
| **Pouring (rain)** | Raining very heavily. | Сильна злива |

## 2. Grammar Essentials

**Past Continuous** використовується для опису тривалих процесів, що відбувалися в певний момент у минулому.

### Formulas:

**Positive:** Subject + was/were + Verb-ing.
 
**Negative:** Subject + was/were + not + Verb-ing.

**Question:** Was/Were + Subject + Verb-ing? 

### Key Usage:

1. 
**Duration:** To show that something continued for some time (e.g., *The rain was pouring all night long*).

2. 
**Specific Moment:** Action happening at a precise time (e.g., *I was climbing the tree yesterday at 10 p.m.*).

3. 
**Simultaneous Actions:** Two or more actions happening at the same time, often connected by **while** (e.g., *I was reading while my husband was watching TV*).


### Spelling Rules (-ing):

 
**-e falls off:** make → making, write → writing.
 
**-ie changes to -y:** die → dying, lie → lying.

**-y remains:** study → studying, play → playing.

**CVC (Double consonant):** if a short verb ends in consonant-vowel-consonant, double the last letter: get → getting, run → running, stop → stopping.

## 3. Written Practice

**Exercise 1: Open the brackets**

1. At 6 p.m. last night, we **were not watching** a movie.

2. They **were playing** football while their mum **was cooking**.

3. I **was reading** a book, and my brother **was listening** to music.

4. Yesterday at noon, he **was not studying**, he **was chatting** with his friends.

5. The children **were running** around the park while their parents **were sitting** on a bench.

**Exercise 2: Sentence Building**

* **Prompt:** The students / prepare / for the exams / at 10 a.m. yesterday.


**Sentence:** The students were preparing for the exams at 10 a.m. yesterday.

* **Prompt:** I / not play / computer games / at midnight. I / study.
**Sentence:** I wasn’t playing computer games at midnight. I was studying.

* **Prompt:** They / swim / in the pool / at 3 p.m. yesterday.

**Sentence:** They were swimming in the pool at 3 p.m. yesterday.

* **Prompt:** We /not travel / to Paris / this time last year.

**Sentence:** We weren’t traveling to Paris this time last year.

**Exercise 3: Error Correction**
1. *Correct:* I **was doing** my homework all day yesterday.

2. *Correct:* What **were** you doing this time yesterday? I was **running**.

3. *Correct:* My friend was studying while I **was watching** TikTok videos.

4. *Correct:* She was preparing for the exams **all night** (or the whole night).

5. *Correct:* What **were we** doing yesterday in the evening? We **were having** dinner.

## 4. Speaking & Discussion Guide

Q1: What were you doing last weekend? 

* **Positive:** To be honest, I had a very productive time. I was mostly working on my personal project and, actually, I was also visiting some old friends on Sunday afternoon.
* **Neutral:** I'm afraid my weekend wasn't too exciting. I was just relaxing at home and, if I remember correctly, I was catching up on some reading.
* **Negative:** I’d like to say I was travelling, but unfortunately, I was feeling a bit under the weather, so I was staying in bed for most of the time.

Q2: What were you doing at 8 PM yesterday? 

* **Positive:** Actually, I was having a lovely dinner with my family. We were discussing our plans for the upcoming holiday.
* **Neutral:** I believe I was simply finishing some chores around the house. It might sound boring, but I was cleaning the kitchen at that time.
* **Negative:** To be perfectly honest, I was still stuck at the office. I was trying to finish a report before the deadline.

Q3: Were you listening to music when you were doing your tasks on the platform? 

* **Positive:** Yes, I certainly was! I find that lo-fi music helps me focus, so I was listening to it while I was working.
* **Negative:** I’m afraid not. I usually prefer complete silence when I'm studying, so I wasn't listening to anything at all.

## 5. Professional Dialogues (Based on Slides)

Scenario: Checking in with a colleague (Based on "What was Ann doing when you called her?" )

* **A:** "I'm so sorry to bother you, but could you tell me why Ann didn't pick up the phone earlier?"
* **B:** "To be honest, I think she **was having** a meeting with the design team when you called. She was actually presenting the new mockups."
* **A:** "Oh, I see. Would it be possible for her to call me back later?"

Scenario: Talking about family (Based on "What were siblings doing while their mom was cooking?" )

* **A:** "Your house seemed quite busy last night. Was everything alright?"
* **B:** "Actually, it was just a normal evening! While my mom **was cooking** dinner, my siblings **were doing** their homework in the living room. It was surprisingly quiet, to be fair."

## 6. Polite English Cheat Sheet

*Використовуйте ці фрази, щоб звучати професійно та ввічливо:*

| Phrase | When to use |
| --- | --- |
| **Actually / To be honest** | Used to introduce a personal opinion or clarify a fact without being too direct. |
| **I'm afraid (not)** | Used to deliver bad news or a negative answer politely. |
| **Could you / Would it be possible...** | Used for making requests in a soft, non-demanding way. |
| **I believe / If I remember correctly** | Used to sound less certain and more humble when stating facts. |
| **To be fair / To be perfectly honest** | Used to add a layer of sincerity to your statement. |
